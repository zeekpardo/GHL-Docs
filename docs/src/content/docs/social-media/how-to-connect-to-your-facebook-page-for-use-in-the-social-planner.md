**How to Connect to Your Facebook Page for Use in the Social Planner**


The Social Planner tool allows you to connect multiple social media accounts and manage them in one area. By connecting Facebook pages, you can use the Social Planner to schedule, create, and push out posts to your Facebook pages, along with Facebook Groups, Instagram, and Google My Business as well.

**NOTE:** If you’ve already connected Facebook pages from a profile and want to add new pages, you will need to remove the application from your Facebook Settings > Business Integration section in Facebook. Then, follow the steps below.

## **Pro Tips:**

-   Ensure you have Admin rights to add your Facebook pages. You have to log yourself in with the same profile which has all the pages you’re an admin for.
-   Facebook’s API requirements only allow you to add pages for posting; you cannot post to personal pages.

### **Step 1: Connect Your Facebook Page(s)**

-   Navigate to Marketing on the left > Social Planner > Settings, or click the Gear Icon on the top right, from there connect to Facebook

### **Note:** No more errors when logging in to connect to your Facebook Page.

We have upgraded to a better version of the Facebook API, which resolves the following issue:

1.  Navigate to Marketing > Social Planner.
2.  Go to Settings.
3.  Connect your Facebook Page.

Previously, while attempting to connect, you might have encountered a message indicating that the page doesn't have adequate permissions. The possible reason for this issue was:

-   The third-party app, LeadConnector, didn't have access to the page.
-   Access was only available from the business manager side.
-   Users with business manager access will be able to connect without issues.

### **FAQ's**




**Question:** Which social media platforms can I connect to the Social Planner?
**Answer:** You can connect Facebook Pages, Facebook Groups, Instagram, and Google My Business accounts.




**Question:** What should I do if I want to add new Facebook Pages after connecting some?
**Answer:** Remove the Social Planner application from Facebook Settings > Business Integration, then reconnect using the standard connection process.




**Question:** What permissions do I need to connect to a Facebook Page?
**Answer:** You must have Admin rights for the page and be logged into the profile associated with those rights.




**Question:** Can I use the Social Planner to post to personal Facebook pages?
**Answer:** No, the tool only supports posting to business pages due to Facebook’s API restrictions.




**Question:** How do I manage my connected social media accounts?
**Answer:** You can manage your accounts by clicking the Settings icon in the Social Planner, where you can add, change, or remove connected pages and groups.




**Question:** Has the Facebook API integration been updated recently?
**Answer:**  Yes. Social Planner uses an upgraded Facebook API, which resolves prior connection errors related to permissions and page access.




**Question:** Does the Social Planner support both the Classic and New Page experiences on Facebook?
**Answer:** Yes, the Social Planner supports both versions. However, if you are using the New Page Experience, you must ensure you have the specific required access roles to connect successfully.




**Question:** Can I connect Facebook Pages from two different personal Facebook profiles?

**Answer:** Yes. You can connect pages from one profile, then log out of Facebook in your browser, log into a different Facebook profile, and follow the connection steps again. The Social Planner allows you to aggregate pages from multiple administrative sources into one dashboard.




**Question:** What should I do if I get a message saying my page doesn't have adequate permissions while trying to connect?

**Answer:** This usually occurs if the third-party app, LeadConnector, does not have access to the page or if access is only available from the Business Manager side. Ensure you have Business Manager access and that the LeadConnector app permissions are enabled to resolve this.
