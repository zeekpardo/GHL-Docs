Ready to actually get some views on Instagram? If you're looking to grow right now, Reels are the way to do it. Here’s how to use the Social Planner to start posting them without the headache.

With reels, you can easily create brief yet captivating videos to connect with your audience and boost your brand's visibility. Showcase your products or services in a fun and creative way, drawing in potential customers and driving traffic to your website.

Don't let this opportunity slip away - start utilizing the power of Instagram Reels with The Social Planner today!

## **Prerequisites**

-   Ensure you have already added an Instagram Professional account \[Publishing Reels to a personal or creator account is NOT available\]
-   Upload Images and videos in the Media Library before creating the reel

**Please Note:** If you’re not sure what type of Instagram profile you have, here’s how to find out:

**Keep in mind that the location and appearance of Instagram settings may vary slightly depending on your device and operating system.**

1\. In the Instagram app, go to your profile, and then select the profile menu in the top right of the screen.

2\. Within Settings and Privacy, scroll down to Account Type and Tools.![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXcruwOK09ztKigHx0PgJJgVrJVCmxDCqT1ojKSAiPntXSm2wv18HtmMSZZGP1c3tG2c7b3bmdCbStC3kDaZUFee7Hpfa5gRtertz00rlaIp4dSeBf3kADYpYIhrcCJxFn_m1PUY?key=sL6C_jhMZiqWlgI56a2Rlw)

3\. Click Switch to professional account and follow the instructions. The options you see there tell you what type of Instagram profile you currently have.

-   Personal profiles show the Switch to Professional Account option.
-   Creator profiles show the Switch account type > Switch to a personal account and Switch to business account options.
-   Business profiles show the Switch account type > Switch to a personal account and Switch to Creator account options.

## **How to make an Instagram Reel using the Social Planner**

### **Step 1: Go to the Social Planner**

 Go to the "Marketing" tab on the left > Social Planner at the top > "New Post."

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1765575385955-image5.jpg)

### **Step 2: Select your Instagram account**

**Note:** If you choose another social account, Reels will not be visible. You must select one or multiple Instagram Professional accounts to publish to Instagram.

### **Step 3: Select Reels as your Instagram content type (See image below)**

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXejZAhmI1-dUW1MuuE3mOYhp1u6h7kxYD5tIMz_UkcHOsUssH2R-TxYItQFfW3Y0s3hVYcmRBTnmVHDcT7_DnTQW-OgkjxYN025KK_aT6UamYmNQNAFYi0qdWPI43NMX3NOQP17MQ?key=sL6C_jhMZiqWlgI56a2Rlw)

### **Step 4: Create your reel by uploading up to 10 images or videos**

### **Step 5: Post or Schedule** 

Select "Post Now" or "Schedule Post". You can also save your story as a draft by selecting Save for Later.

If your post was scheduled, you will see your reel under the "**schedule posts**" tab

### **Supported Content for Instagram Reels**

-   Suggested video size up to 1GB and for thumbnail is 8MB.
-   Aspect Ratio - The dimension should be recommended at 9:16.
-   Duration: 15 mins maximum and 3 seconds minimum
-   Format: MOV or MP4

**Collaborator Posts for Instagram Feeds & Reels**

Collaborator posts allow users to co-author Instagram content with another account, making it visible on both profiles’ Feeds or Reels. This feature helps boost engagement, extend audience reach, and strengthen partnerships by sharing visibility.

To use this feature, create a post, select Instagram Feed or Reel, and enter a valid Instagram username in the collaborator field. Ensure the Instagram account is properly connected in settings.

### **Share to Feed Toggle**

The “Share to Feed” toggle gives creators simple control over where their short-form videos appear, letting them choose whether a reel shows on both the main feed and the reels tab or only on the reels tab. This helps maintain a cleaner profile look while supporting more flexible posting styles. 

To use it, just switch the toggle on or off when preparing your reel. This option functions within ’s posting workflow and may be limited by the display rules supported by the connected account.

**Key Features & Benefits**

**Increased visibility:** Instagram Reels allow users to reach a wider audience. When you create a Reel, it has the potential to be featured on the Explore page, where people who don't follow you can discover your content. This exposure can help individuals, influencers, and businesses gain more visibility and attract new followers.

**Creative expression:** Reels offer a platform for users to showcase their creativity and talent. With various editing tools, effects, filters, and audio options, people can experiment and create engaging visually appealing short videos.

**Discoverability:** Instagram Reels' algorithm shows users content based on interests, preferences, and engagement patterns. This means that if you consistently create engaging Reels, your content is more likely to be recommended to users likely to enjoy it. This can help individuals and brands increase their reach and attract more followers.

**Business promotion:** Reels also allow businesses to humanize their brand and connect with their audience more personally.

**Bite-sized content consumption:** Short-form content is popular in today's fast-paced digital environment due to its quick and easily consumable nature. Users can scroll through Reels quickly, making it a convenient way to discover and engage with content.

## FAQs




**Question:** Can I use Instagram Reels with any Instagram account?

**Answer:** No, Reels can only be used with Instagram Professional accounts. Reels will not be visible for publishing if you select another type of account.




**Question:** What types of content can I include in an Instagram Reel?

**Answer:** Using The Social Planner, you can include up to 10 images or videos in your Instagram Reel.




**Question:** Is there a time limit for Instagram Reels?

**Answer:** Yes, Instagram Reels have a duration limit. Your reel should be between 3 seconds (minimum) and 15 minutes (maximum) long.




**Question:** Can I schedule my Instagram Reel for later publication?

**Answer:** Yes, you can schedule your Instagram Reel for later publication using The Social Planner. After creating your reel, you can choose "Save for Later," and it will be saved for future posting.




**Question:** Can I use Instagram Reels for personal or creator accounts?

**Answer:** No, Instagram Reels can only be published from Instagram Professional accounts. Personal or creator accounts do not have access to this feature.




**Question:** How can Instagram Reels help me connect with my audience?

**Answer:** Instagram Reels provide a fun and creative way to showcase your products or services, making engaging and connecting with your audience through visual storytelling easier.




**Question:** Is there a limit to how many Instagram Reels I can create using The Social Planner?

**Answer:** There is no specific limit to the number of Instagram Reels you can create using The Social Planner. You can use the feature as often as you like to engage your audience and promote your brand.




**Question:** What type of Instagram profile do I need to use Instagram Reels with The Social Planner?

**Answer:** To use Instagram Reels with The Social Planner, you must have a Professional Instagram account (either a Creator or Business account). Reels will not be available for personal accounts. You can switch to a Professional account directly through your Instagram settings if needed.  




**Question:** How do "Collaborator Posts" work in the Social Planner?

**Answer:** When you add a collaborator's username to your post, the Reel will be sent to them for approval. Once they accept, the video appears on **both** your profile and theirs, sharing all the likes, comments, and views. It’s an excellent way to partner with influencers or other brands.




**Question:** Can I edit a Reel after it has been scheduled?
**Answer:** Yes. You can find your scheduled Reel in the "Scheduled Posts" tab. Click the menu dots and select "Edit" to change the caption, collaborator, or video before it goes live.
