Since LinkedIn caches the content for 7 days, if you edit, and share the URL again, they will still see the older version.

## FAQ’s




**Question:** Why would I need to clear the cache of a LinkedIn preview link?

**Answer:** You may need to clear the cache of a LinkedIn preview link if you have shared a URL with an incorrect title, description, or image. LinkedIn caches this information for 7 days, so even if you update the content, the old preview may still appear unless the cache is cleared.




**Question:** How long does LinkedIn cache the content of a shared URL?

**Answer:** LinkedIn caches the content of a shared URL for 7 days. During this time, any updates to the title, description, or image of the URL will not be reflected in the preview unless the cache is cleared.




**Question:** How can I clear the cache of my LinkedIn preview link?

**Answer:** To clear the cache of your LinkedIn preview link, visit [LinkedIn Post Inspector](https://www.linkedin.com/post-inspector/), enter your URL, and click on Inspect. This will refresh the preview data and display the updated title, description, or image.




**Question:** What is a LinkedIn Post Inspector?

**Answer:** LinkedIn Post Inspector is a tool provided by LinkedIn that allows you to preview how your URL will look when shared on LinkedIn. It also refreshes the cached data for the URL, ensuring that any updates to the content are reflected in the preview.




**Question:** What should I do if my LinkedIn link preview still shows old information after updating the content?

**Answer:** If your LinkedIn link preview still shows old information, use LinkedIn Post Inspector to refresh the cache. Enter your URL at [LinkedIn Post Inspector](https://www.linkedin.com/post-inspector/), click Inspect, and the tool will update the preview data to reflect the latest changes.




**Question:** Can I update the LinkedIn preview link without waiting 7 days for the cache to refresh?

**Answer:** Yes, you can update the LinkedIn preview link immediately by using LinkedIn Post Inspector. This tool clears the cache and updates the preview so that any changes to the title, description, or image are reflected right away.




**Question:** What information does LinkedIn cache when a URL is shared?

**Answer:** When a URL is shared on LinkedIn, the platform caches the title, description, and image associated with the link. This cached information is used to generate the preview that appears when the link is shared.




**Question:** What happens if I don’t clear the cache of my LinkedIn preview link?

**Answer:** If you don't clear the cache of your LinkedIn preview link, LinkedIn will continue to show the outdated title, description, or image even if you've updated them. Since LinkedIn caches the preview data for 7 days, the changes will not appear until the cache is manually refreshed using the LinkedIn Post Inspector tool.




**Question:** Is LinkedIn Post Inspector safe to use?

**Answer:** Yes. It’s an official LinkedIn tool designed to preview links and refresh cached data.




**Question:** Does the Post Inspector tell me why my image looks blurry?

**Answer:** Yes. When you click "Inspect," scroll down to the **"URL Information"** or **"Metadata"** section. LinkedIn will often give you a warning if your image is too small or if the dimensions are not ideal for their feed.




**Question:** My title is being cut off in the preview; what is the character limit?

**Answer:** LinkedIn typically displays up to **150 characters** for titles. If your title is longer, the Post Inspector will show you where the truncation occurs so you can edit your website's metadata accordingly.




**Question:** Why does LinkedIn show the old version of my page even after I’ve fixed it on my website?

**Answer:** LinkedIn **caches content for 7 days**. This means they store a "snapshot" of your link’s data to save time, and they won't look for updates automatically until that week is over unless you use the Post Inspector.
