**Question:** Why is my call quality poor, and how can I fix it?
**Answer:** If your calls sound choppy or unclear, the issue may be poor internet connectivity. To improve it:

1.  Test your internet speed using a reliable speed test tool (e.g., speed.cloudflare.com).
2.  Ensure your connection has at least 50Mbps for both download and upload speeds, a jitter under 10ms, and a ping below 40ms. If your connection is below these standards, consider upgrading your plan or router.

**How to Test:**

1\. Visit [speed.cloudflare.com](https://speed.cloudflare.com) to check your connection speed.

**Question:** What does 'high-jitter' mean, and how can I fix it?
**Answer:** Jitter refers to the variation in time between data packets arriving at their destination. High jitter can cause audio distortion, like crackling or choppy speech. To fix this:

1.  Ensure that your router is not overloaded with other tasks.
2.  If you're using Wi-Fi, switch to a wired connection for more stable performance.
3.  Check your router's settings and consider adjusting the Quality of Service (QoS) settings to prioritize VoIP traffic.

**How to Use:**

1.  Connect your device directly to the router using an Ethernet cable.
2.  If you're using Wi-Fi, ensure that you are close to the router and that there are minimal obstructions.




**Question:** What is 'high-packet-loss,' and how can I resolve it?
**Answer:** Packet loss occurs when data packets fail to reach their destination. It can cause poor call quality and even drop calls. To address packet loss:

1.  Test your network using [test.8x8.vc](https://test.8x8.vc/) to check for compatibility with VoIP.
2.  If packet loss is detected, check if other devices are consuming too much bandwidth and consider disconnecting them during important calls.
3.  Ensure that your router firmware is up to date, and consider using a higher-quality router if needed.

**How to Test:**

1.  Visit [test.8x8.vc](https://test.8x8.vc/) to see if your network is compatible with VoIP.
2.  If packet loss is detected, contact your internet provider for troubleshooting.

**Question:** How can I ensure my mobile app doesn't experience call quality issues?

**Answer:

1.  Check for 'high-jitter' or 'high-packet-loss' warnings within the app. These indicators will let you know what might be wrong.
2.  Move to a location with a stronger signal if you're on Wi-Fi or cellular data.
3.  Try using the app on a different network to see if the issue persists.

**How to Use:**Open your mobile app and check for any call quality warnings, such as "high-jitter" or "high-packet-loss."  
If warnings appear, move closer to the router or switch to a more stable network.  




**Question:** Why do my calls drop frequently, and how can I prevent it?
**Answer:** Frequent call drops are often caused by unstable network connections, bandwidth limitations, or router configuration issues. To minimize dropped calls:

-   Ensure your internet connection meets the required stability and speed standards for voice communication.  

-   Limit background activities such as large downloads or streaming during calls.  

-   Restart your router periodically to refresh the connection and clear temporary network errors.  

-   If using Wi-Fi, move closer to the router or switch to a wired connection for more stability.  


**How to Use:**Monitor your network strength during calls. If calls frequently disconnect, test your internet speed, close unnecessary background applications, and reconnect to a more stable network before retrying.




**Question:** Why does my call have an echo, and how do I fix it?
**Answer:** Echo usually happens when your microphone picks up the sound from your speakers. To fix this:

-   **Use headphones instead of speakers.**
-   **Lower your speaker volume.**
-   **Check that only one microphone and one speaker source are active on your device.**

**How to Fix:**

1.  **Plug in wired or Bluetooth headphones.**
2.  **Go to your device's audio settings and ensure the correct mic and speaker are selected.**




**Question:** Can my browser extensions affect call quality?
**Answer:** Yes. Certain ad-blockers or security extensions can block the scripts required for the dialer to connect to the audio server.

-   **How to Test:** Open your application in an Incognito or Private window. If the call quality improves or the connection issues disappear, one of your browser extensions is the culprit.
