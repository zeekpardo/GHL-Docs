**Ad Manager: Modifying a Meta Ad Campaign in Published or Review Status**

In the dynamic world of digital advertising, the ability to edit a live campaign is crucial. This functionality allows advertisers to respond to changing market conditions, optimize performance, and update content without disrupting the ongoing ad spend. In this article, we explore the key features and steps involved in editing published or in-review campaigns using a generic Ad Manager, providing practical tips and answering common questions.

## **Key Features and Benefits**

### **Flexibility and Control**

-   **Edit on the Fly:** Adjust various elements like text, media, and targeting while the campaign is live.
-   **Adapt to Market Changes:** Quickly respond to market trends and feedback to maximize campaign effectiveness.
-   **Optimize Budget:** Modify budget allocations based on performance metrics.

### **Efficiency**

-   **Avoid Campaign Setup Redo:** Make necessary changes without starting from scratch.
-   **Real-Time Updates:** Apply edits immediately, allowing for agile marketing adjustments.

### **Fields Editable in a Live Campaign**

-   **Campaign Name and CTA Buttons**
-   **Lead Generation Forms and Website URLs**
-   **Ad Category, Placements, and Media**
-   **Budget Adjustments and Targeting Options**

### **Fields Not Editable**

-   **Campaign Objective**
-   **Associated Ad Accounts**
-   **Start Dates and Budget Type (from Daily to Lifetime)**

## FAQs




**Question:** Can I change the campaign objective once it's live?
**Answer:** _No, the campaign objective is fixed once set and cannot be altered during live operation._




**Question:** How quickly do changes take effect once published?
**Answer:** _Changes usually go live immediately after approval but may vary depending on the review process._




**Question:** Is there a limit to how many times I can edit a live campaign?
**Answer:** _No specific limit, but frequent changes may lead to review delays and could impact campaign performance._




**Question:** Can I revert to the original settings after making edits?
**Answer:** _Yes, if you haven't published the changes, you can discard them and revert to the original settings._




**Question:** What should I do if my edited campaign fails the review process?
**Answer:** _Review the feedback, make necessary adjustments, and resubmit. Common issues often relate to compliance with advertising standards._




**Question:** Will editing a live campaign disrupt ongoing ad delivery?

**Answer:** No, edits are applied without stopping the campaign, allowing you to optimize performance without interrupting ad spend.




**Question:** What happens to my ad during the "Review" period after I make an edit?

**Answer:** In most cases, your original ad continues to run while the new version is being reviewed. Once the review is approved, the system seamlessly swaps the old version for the new one. This ensures you don't lose any "up-time" or potential leads while waiting.




**Question:** If I change the ad media (image/video), will I lose the likes and comments on the original ad?

**Answer:** Yes. When you swap out the media file, the platform treats it as a "new" creative. The social proof (likes, shares, comments) from the previous version will not carry over to the new image or video.




**Question:** Is it possible to switch my budget from a "Daily Budget" to a "Lifetime Budget" once the campaign has started?

**Answer:** No. While you can adjust the **amount** of your budget, the **Budget Type** (Daily vs. Lifetime) and the **Start Date** are fixed and cannot be edited once the campaign is live.
