Launching a branded mobile app is a crucial step in solidifying your business’s presence and offering a seamless experience to your customers. Whether you're looking to engage users or enhance your brand, following a structured process can help ensure the app creation and submission go smoothly. This guide takes you through each stage, from payment to going live, while providing real-time progress tracking to keep you on track.

## **Key Features and Benefits**

-   **Real-Time Progress Tracking**: Color-coded steps help you monitor the progress of your app’s launch in real time.
-   **Personalized Branding**: Customize your app to align with your brand’s identity, ensuring a unique and cohesive user experience.
-   **In-App Notifications**: Receive timely updates on your app’s status, ensuring you're always informed.
-   **Easy Submission**: Once the necessary details are finalized, submitting your app to the app store is straightforward.

## **Prerequisites for App Development**

**D-U-N-S Number:**To obtain an organizational developer account, a D-U-N-S Number is required. You can apply for one through the official D-U-N-S website. If the application process is taking longer than expected, you can reach out to D-U-N-S support to potentially expedite the process.

**Organizational Developer Accounts:**For app development to proceed, organizational developer accounts are necessary. Please ensure you provide the necessary developer accounts from both Google and Apple.

-   **Google Developer Account:**Make sure that your Google developer account is verified. If the account verification is incomplete, apps will not be eligible for publishing. Should you encounter issues while setting up the account, please reach out to Google Support for assistance.
-   **Apple Developer Account:**Once your Apple Developer account is created, log in and visit the App Store Connect to complete the required legal documentation. If you plan to distribute apps in the EU, register as an EU trader by selecting "I am a trader" under the Digital Services Act (DSA), which will require additional documentation. If EU distribution is not relevant to you, simply select "No, I am not a trader." Should you face difficulties while setting up the Apple Developer account, contact Apple Support for help in expediting or resolving the process.

**App Description and Content Guidelines:**It is important that all app descriptions, carousel image descriptions, carousel images, and splash screen images do not contain financial terms such as "paid," "free," or "premium subscription." Additionally, these terms should not appear in URLs, including those for support, terms and conditions, or privacy policy pages. Non-compliance with these guidelines may prevent the app from being published.

### **Step 07: Book a Slot for 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication)**

**Description**: Once your app passes review, you will need to schedule a time for two-factor authentication (2FA) to ensure app security.  
**How to Use**:

-   **Select a time slot** that works best for you. During this slot, you will receive a 2FA code either by **email or SMS**.
-   Ensure that you are available to enter the code in real-time to continue the process.
-   Once you complete the 2FA process, the status will update to "App Submitted to Stores."

### **Step 08: App Submitted to Stores**

**Description**: This step marks the final submission of your app to the app stores. The app store review process will now begin, which may take several days depending on the platform.  
**How to Use**:

-   No action is needed at this stage.
-   **Wait for notifications** from the app store for updates on your submission status.
-   You will be notified once your app is **approved** and **live** on the App Store.

## **Additional Tips for a Smooth Launch**

-   **Review Details Thoroughly**: Be sure to thoroughly review your branding and profile information before submitting. Since changes cannot be made post-submission, ensure everything is correct up front.
-   **Stay Updated**: Regularly check your email and in-app notifications for updates or additional requests from the review team. The faster you respond, the smoother the process will be.
-   **Prepare for 2FA**: Plan your schedule accordingly when booking a slot for two-factor authentication. Ensure you’re available to quickly input the authentication code.

## **FAQ's**




**Question:** What should I do if I notice an error after submitting my details?
**Answer:** Once submitted, you cannot make changes to your details. Therefore, always double-check your branding and profile before finalizing the submission. If the review fails, you’ll be allowed to fix issues, and then you can resubmit.




**Question:** How long does the review process take?
**Answer:** The review process typically takes around 5 business days. However, if there are any issues with your submission, it may take longer. Keep an eye out for any additional feedback.




**Question:** Can I change my branding after submission?
**Answer:** No, once you submit your branding details, they are locked in and cannot be altered. Be sure everything is correct before clicking the Submit button.




**Question:** What if the app review is unsuccessful?  
**Answer:** If your app’s review fails, the reason for the failure will be provided. You must fix the issues and resubmit. Common issues include missing branding elements or incorrect profile details.




**Question:** How will I know when my app is live?  
Answer**: Once the app has been reviewed and approved by the app store, you will receive a notification that your app is live and ready to be shared with users.


**Question:  How long does it take for my app to go live after submission?
**Answer:** It usually takes about 3–7 business days for Google Play and 5–10 business days for the Apple App Store to review your app. You’ll get notified once it’s approved and live.




**Question:** Can I track the progress of my app launch?
**Answer:** Yes. provides **real-time progress tracking** with color-coded steps to monitor each stage from payment to submission.




**Question:** How will I be notified about app status updates?
**Answer:** Updates are sent via in-app notifications and email. Make sure your notification preferences are enabled to stay informed.




**Question:** What is a D-U-N-S Number and why do I need one?

**Answer:** A D-U-N-S Number is a unique nine-digit identifier for businesses. It is a mandatory requirement for obtaining an **organizational developer account** (as opposed to a personal one). You can apply for one through the official D-U-N-S website.




**Question:** My Google Developer account is create, why is my app still not publishing?
**Answer:** Ensure that your Google developer account is **fully verified**. If the account verification is incomplete, Google will not allow the app to be eligible for publishing.
