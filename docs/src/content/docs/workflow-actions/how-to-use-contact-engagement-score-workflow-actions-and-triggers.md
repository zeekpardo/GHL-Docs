## Understanding the Contact Engagement Score

Before diving into the workflow actions and triggers, it's essential to understand the Contact Engagement Score and its significance. The Contact Engagement Score is a numerical representation of a contact's engagement level with your platform or brand. It takes into account various interactions such as email opens, appointment bookings, tag additions, and more, allowing you to gauge the level of interest and activity of each contact.

## Setting Up Engagement Scoring

### FAQ: Contact Engagement Score Workflow




**Question:** What is the Contact Engagement Score and how is it calculated?
**Answer: The Contact Engagement Score is a numerical representation of a contact's level of engagement with your brand or platform. It is calculated based on predefined rules and actions, such as email opens, clicks, appointment bookings, and tag additions. Each interaction can either add or subtract points, reflecting the contact’s overall engagement.




**Question:** How can I set up a Contact Engagement Score Workflow in ?
**Answer:** To set up a Contact Engagement Score Workflow, go to the Automation section in your platform. Create a new workflow and add the "Contact Engagement Score" trigger. Define conditions for the trigger, such as when the engagement score reaches a certain threshold, and then select the actions you want the workflow to perform, like sending an email or updating a contact’s status.




**Question:** Can I customize the scoring rules for my contacts?
**Answer: Yes, you can customize scoring rules in the "Manage Scoring" section within the settings of . You can define specific actions that add or subtract points from a contact's score, allowing you to tailor the scoring system to reflect the engagement activities that matter most to your business.




**Question:** How do I use engagement scores to segment my contacts?
**Answer:** Engagement scores can be used to segment contacts by setting up filters based on score ranges. For example, you can create segments for highly engaged contacts, moderately engaged contacts, and inactive contacts. This allows you to target your marketing and engagement efforts more effectively, tailoring your communications to each segment’s level of engagement.




**Question:** What actions can be triggered by changes in the Contact Engagement Score?
**Answer:** You can set up workflows to trigger a variety of actions based on changes in a contact's engagement score. These actions include sending personalized emails, triggering SMS messages, assigning tags, scheduling follow-up tasks, or adding contacts to specific marketing campaigns. The flexibility of these triggers allows you to automate responses and engagement strategies effectively.




**Question:** Can engagement scores be used for data management?
**Answer:** Yes, engagement scores help identify inactive contacts, clean your data, and maintain a focused and effective contact list for campaigns.




**Question:** Can I see a contact's engagement score while I am messaging them?

**Answer:** Yes. Within the **Conversations Module**, users have an "at-a-glance" view of contact scores, allowing you to identify high-priority contacts during live interactions.
