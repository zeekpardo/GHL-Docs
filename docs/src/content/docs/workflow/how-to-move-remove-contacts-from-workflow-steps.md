In this article, you will learn how to move or remove contacts from workflow steps. This can be useful in case these contacts were added by mistake, and to this workflow, or if you no longer want them to wait to move them to another step. 

### Step 1: Access your Workflow

-   Navigate to Automation > Workflows in .
-   Choose a workflow that has contacts you want to remove.

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764682349126-unnamed - 2025-12-02T213221.852.jpg>)

### Option 1: Delete from the action step

-   If a workflow step has a contact on that step, there will be a user icon on the top part of that step. If you click on that icon, a list will pop up that shows all the contacts waiting in that step. 

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764682371845-unnamed - 2025-12-02T213243.474.jpg>)

-   Click on that contact(s) 
-   That will show the contacts that live in that step 

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764682440806-unnamed - 2025-12-02T213351.465.jpg>)

**Trash Can:** The contact will be removed from the workflow entirely 

**Running Man:** The contact will be moved to the next step in the workflow

**NOTE:** If you click one of these buttons, that action will occur for ALL the contacts in that step.

**Option 2: Delete from the Enrollment History tab**

-   If you want to delete a single contact from a workflow or move that contact to another step, go to the _Enrollment History tab_ at the top of the builder view.

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764682465646-unnamed - 2025-12-02T213418.525.png>)

This will show you a list of all contacts in this workflow as well as the current step they are in.  

Next to the name and email of the contact, you can click on the **Delete** button which will remove the contact from the workflow, or the **Move to Next Step** button to move that contact to the next step in the workflow.

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764682486996-unnamed - 2025-12-02T213439.899.png>)

### FAQs




**Question:** Can I remove a single contact from a specific step without affecting others in that step?
**Answer:** Yes, to remove a single contact, use the Enrollment History tab in the workflow builder. This allows you to delete or move an individual contact without impacting other contacts in the same step.




**Question:** What happens if I click the Trash Can or Running Man icons on a workflow step?
**Answer:** Clicking the Trash Can icon will remove all contacts from that step and the workflow entirely. Clicking the Running Man icon will move all contacts in that step to the next step of the workflow.




**Question:** Is there a way to view all contacts currently in a workflow and their respective steps?
**Answer:** Yes, the Enrollment History tab provides a complete list of all contacts in the workflow along with the specific step they are currently in, allowing you to manage their movement or removal.




**Question:** Can I undo the removal of a contact from a workflow step?
**Answer:** No, once a contact is removed from a workflow step or the entire workflow, the action cannot be undone. You would need to re-enroll the contact into the workflow manually if needed.




**Question:** How can I delete contacts from a workflow step?

**Answer:** Click on the user icon at the top of the step, select the contacts, and click the trash can to delete them.




**Question:** What is the difference between the Trash Can icon and the Running Man icon?

**Answer:** The Trash Can icon removes contacts from the workflow entirely, while the Running Man icon moves contacts forward to the next step in the workflow.




**Question:** Will removing a contact from a workflow delete them from my Contacts list?

**Answer:** No. Removing a contact from a workflow step only stops the automation for them. Their contact record, tags, and history remain intact within your main Contacts database.




**Question:** Does "Deleting" a contact from a workflow also stop their pending SMS or Emails?

**Answer:** Yes. The moment a contact is removed from a workflow, all future actions including scheduled emails, text messages, and wait periods are cancelled instantly for that person.
