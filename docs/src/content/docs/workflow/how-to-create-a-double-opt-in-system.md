Double Opt-In Systems serve multiple purposes. First and foremost, it is used in the EU and required by GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation). This means that once a lead opts-in, they must confirm the opt-in to continue receiving information. The Double Opt-In System can also be used to request a verification of information such as an email address. To create a double opt-in, here's what you can do: 

### Step 1:

Use a triggering action (such as Form Submitted) to have the original opt-in send them through a Campaign or Workflow series, requesting that they confirm the opt-in. This can be a follow-up confirmation email, text, or both.

### Step 2:

Within that communication, include a trigger link for them to confirm.

**_NOTE:_** Check out our tutorials on creating Trigger Links if you need assistance.

### Step 3: 

Set up a trigger action so that when the “Confirm Opt In Trigger Link” (whatever you named that which you created in Step 2) is clicked, the rest of the communications/interactions take place (i.e. any nurture campaign sequences, tag applications, pipeline changes, workflows, etc.)

### FAQ 




**Question:** What is a Double Opt-In System, and why is it important?
**Answer:** A Double Opt-In System is used primarily to comply with regulations like GDPR in the EU. It requires leads to confirm their opt-in before receiving further communication. It also verifies contact details, such as email addresses.




**Question:** How do I create a Trigger Link for the confirmation?
**Answer:** You can create a Trigger Link and name it (e.g., "Confirm Opt-In") in your platform settings. Refer to our tutorials for step-by-step instructions on creating Trigger Links.




**Question:** What happens after the lead confirms their opt-in?
**Answer:** Once the Trigger Link is clicked, the rest of your planned communications and actions (e.g., nurturing campaigns, tag applications, or workflow sequences) will automatically be triggered.




**Question:** Is Double Opt-In mandatory?
**Answer:** In certain regions like the EU, Double Opt-In is required by law under GDPR for collecting and processing lead information. In other areas, it is optional but beneficial for verifying contact data.




**Question:** Can Double Opt-In be used for both email and SMS?
**Answer:** Yes. You can request confirmation through email, SMS, or both, depending on your campaign setup.




**Question:** How does the Double Opt-In System comply with GDPR requirements?
**Answer:** The Double Opt-In System is required by GDPR in the EU, as it ensures that leads explicitly confirm their consent to receive communications, helping to protect personal data. This process allows businesses to verify contact details and obtain clear, documented consent before sending further marketing or communication materials.




**Question:** How can I create a double opt-in workflow or campaign?
**Answer:** You can create a double opt-in workflow by setting up a trigger action (e.g., Form Submitted) that sends the lead through a series of communications asking them to confirm their opt-in. This can include emails or texts with a confirmation link, which, when clicked, will trigger the next steps in your workflow, such as nurturing campaigns or tag applications.  




**Question:** Does a Double Opt-In system help reduce my spam rate?
**Answer:** Yes, significantly. Because users must physically click a link in their inbox to confirm, it prevents bots and fake email addresses from entering your system. This leads to higher open rates and protects your sender reputation with providers like Gmail and Outlook.




**Question:** What specific element must be included in the confirmation email or text?

**Answer:** You must include a "Trigger Link" within the communication. This link acts as the bridge that the lead must click to verify their intent and move to the next stage of your funnel.
