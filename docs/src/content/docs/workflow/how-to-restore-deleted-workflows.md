**How to Restore Deleted Workflows**

Restoring deleted workflows is a crucial feature that allows users to recover workflows that have been removed. This feature ensures that important processes can be restored, preventing the loss of valuable data and configurations.

## **How to Restore Deleted Workflows**

### **Step-by-Step Guide**

**1\. Navigate to the Workflow List Page**

-   Go to Automation > Workflows

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763674934896-image5.png)

**2\. Access the "Deleted" Tab**

-   On the Workflow list page, click on the newly added "Deleted" tab to view all deleted workflows.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXcXd988JcDdI2lRRt0iQHKgV3gQ4zgsJd5LVCAk4k6Q9a-CN6ewJpVGO-n1azrxl4UAq8aNiYBka5RwtzeG5Nfi_-xLdGf42OH2tJAjzLSCG4jDdGfdsOdphSiXoJRryQRGYB4QIuewEC1KINJSuwq0zd81?key=mJDTK2k4F4RRuesBZ-AxMg)

**3\. Select the Workflow to Restore**

-   Browse through the list of deleted workflows and locate the one you want to restore.
-   Click on the "Restore" button next to the workflow.

## **Note**

-   Restored workflows will be in the Draft stage upon restoration. Ensure to review and publish them if necessary.
-   Contacts in the wait step will be removed from the workflow execution upon restoration.
-   The workflow will retain its original triggers and actions, but any contacts in the workflow when it was deleted will not be restored.
-   Deleted workflows will remain accessible for 90 days. Once a workflow is restored, it cannot be deleted again for the next 48 hours. If you attempt to delete it within this period, you will receive a notification informing you of the restriction.

## FAQs




**Question:** For how long will the deleted workflows be present in the "Deleted" tab?
**Answer:** The workflows will be removed from the "Deleted" tab after 90 days.




**Question:** Can I delete the workflow I just restored?

**Answer:** No, the restored workflows can't be deleted for the next 48 hours.




**Question:** Will my workflow be in a published state post restoring?

**Answer:** No, upon successful restoration, the workflow will be placed in the Draft stage.




**Question:** Will all the information be available in the workflow after restoring it?
**Answer:** Upon restoring a workflow, the contact information in enrollment history and execution logs will stay intact. However, contacts in the wait step will be removed, and any contacts in the workflow when it was deleted will not be restored. The restored workflow will retain its original triggers and actions with the same configuration.




**Question:** Why can’t I delete a restored workflow immediately?
**Answer:** To prevent accidental data loss, restored workflows cannot be deleted again for 48 hours. A notification will appear if you attempt deletion during this period.




**Question:** Does the workflow start running immediately after I restore it?

**Answer:** No. To ensure you have a chance to review the settings, every restored workflow is placed in the “**Draft”** stage. You must manually review and click "**Publish**" to make it active again.




**Question:** What is the first thing I should do after clicking "Restore"?

**Answer:** Since the workflow returns to a **Draft** stage, your first step should be to review the configuration and manually **Publish** it if you want it to become active again.
