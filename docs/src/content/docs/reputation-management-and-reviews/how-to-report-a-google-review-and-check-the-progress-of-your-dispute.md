**How to Report a Google Review and Check the Progress of Your Dispute**

Online reviews are essential for your business's reputation, but sometimes, a review may not be genuine, fair, or may violate Google’s guidelines. In such cases, it’s important to know how to dispute a Google review to ensure your business profile remains accurate and trustworthy. This article will guide you on how to report a Google review, track the progress of your dispute, and understand the process better.

### **When to Report a Google Review**

Before diving into the steps, it’s important to understand when you should report a review. You can dispute a review if:

-   **It violates Google’s guidelines**, such as posting spam, offensive language, or irrelevant content.
-   **It is fake or biased**, and does not reflect a genuine customer experience.
-   **It contains misleading or inappropriate content**.

When flagged as inappropriate, Google may take action against the reviewer, which could include removing the review or even banning them from posting future reviews. However, this is usually only done if the review clearly violates Google’s policies.

### **Reporting a Google Review from within the Conversations Tab**

To report a Google review directly from the **Conversations** tab in your , follow these steps:

#### **Step 1: Access the Conversations Tab**

-   Log into your and go to the **Conversations** tab.

### **Checking the Status of Reviews You Have Reported**

If you’ve already reported a review and want to see its current status, follow these steps:

#### **Step 1: Check Status**

-   Go to the **Reviews** section and click on **Check the status of a reported review**. You will be shown a list of reviews that you’ve reported, including their current status.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXf33vAFtkuhOoUBfYCtFLtDKsrMvVqJH1H3P-E-Tl1Ig9k2-wjVocXVkKywb0D-RLogbGYPWNoJzkgLHyRg7ByzxBMVml_2JIeoswFhQnbOqFXP7g7eSRA9sBj55IZgrYw6D5QJ?key=iLelf9udHYGUDN02As-5781f)

#### **Step 2: Review Status Details**

-   Click on any reported review to see more details. This will provide you with a summary of the review's status.
-   If your **takedown request** was rejected, you can submit an **appeal** to contest the decision.

### **Important Notes to Keep in Mind**

-   **Limitations on Listings**: There is a limit to the number of reviews that Google will show for review disputes. For Google My Business accounts that manage a large number of listings, this feature may not be available.
-   **Account Limitations:** According to Joy Hawkins, the feature may not be accessible for accounts with numerous business listings. In cases where an account manages a large number of profiles, such as over 30 listings, Google may prevent access to the dispute process.

### **FAQs**




**Question:** How do I report a Google review?
**Answer:** To report a Google review, go to the Conversations tab in your . Search for the contact associated with the review you want to dispute, then click Dispute this review. You will be prompted to confirm your Google email and the business account linked to the review in question. From there, you can either report a new review or check the status of a previously reported review.




**Question:** What types of reviews can I report?
**Answer:** You can report reviews that violate Google’s guidelines, such as:

Spam or promotional content

Offensive or inappropriate language

Fake reviews or biased content that doesn't reflect an actual customer experience

Reviews that are irrelevant to your business




**Question:** Can I report a review that was not generated by the system?
**Answer:** If the review was not generated by the system, you will need to start the reporting process separately. Please follow the instructions provided on the platform to dispute a non-system-generated review.




**Question:** How do I check the status of a review I’ve already reported?
**Answer:** To check the status of a previously reported review, go to the Reviews section in your dashboard and select Check the status of a reported review. You’ll see a list of reviews you've flagged and their current status. If the takedown request was not accepted, you can submit an appeal.




**Question:** Can I appeal if my takedown request was denied?
**Answer:** Yes, if your takedown request was rejected, you can appeal the decision by clicking the "Submit Appeal" option. This allows you to provide additional information or clarification to Google for further review.




**Question:** What happens after I report a review as inappropriate?  
**Answer:** Once you report a review, Google may investigate the review and take action. If the review violates Google’s policies, it could be removed or the reviewer could be banned from posting future reviews. Google does not guarantee immediate removal, but they will evaluate whether the review violates their guidelines.




**Question:** How long does it take for Google to take action?
**Answer:** The time varies. Google reviews each case according to their guidelines, so resolution may take several days.  




**Question:** What should I do if a review contains misleading or inappropriate content?
**Answer:** If a review contains misleading or inappropriate content, you can report it through the Conversations tab in your . Follow the steps outlined to dispute the review. If the review violates Google’s guidelines, such as posting misleading information or inappropriate language, Google may take action, including removal or banning the reviewer from posting future reviews.




**Question:** Can I dispute multiple reviews at once?
**Answer:** While you can report reviews one by one from the Conversations tab, Google's official **Review Management Tool** allows you to select up to 10 reviews for a bulk appeal if they were all previously rejected.




**Question:** Why is the dispute feature not appearing for my account?

**Answer:** This feature may be restricted if your Google Business Profile account manages a **large number of listings**. Specifically, Google often prevents access to the dispute process for accounts managing **more than 30 profiles**.




**Question:** What are the three specific conditions under which Google allows you to dispute a review?

**Answer:** You can dispute a review if it **violates Google’s guidelines** (spam or offensive language), if it is **fake or biased** and doesn't reflect a genuine experience, or if it contains **misleading or inappropriate content**.
