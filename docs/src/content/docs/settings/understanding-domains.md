**Understanding Domains**

Domains are indispensable assets for your business, providing a streamlined way for people to access essential information about your business, such as your website. The beauty of domains lies in their simplicity; once set up, users can effortlessly connect with your online presence without requiring any additional effort from your end. While the intricacies of domains might not be necessary for everyone to master, we've compiled a quick list of tips and frequently asked questions to guide you through the process of setting up your website or funnel.

## **Domains vs Subdomains**

**Domains:**

_Primary Identity_: Your main web address (e.g., [www.yourbusiness.com](http://www.yourbusiness.com)).

_Independence_: Operates as a standalone entity.

_Brand Presence_: Critical for establishing a professional and unique online identity.

**Subdomains**:

_Extension of the Main Domain_: Functions as a branch of your primary domain (e.g., blog.yourbusiness.com).

_Organizational Structure_: Useful for categorizing or organizing specific content.

_Separate Identities_: These can represent different departments or functionalities within your business.

When setting up your online presence, carefully consider whether you need additional subdomains to organize your content effectively or if a single, powerful domain is sufficient for your business needs.

### **WWW vs Non-WWW Domains**

Did you know that [www.mywebsite.com](http://www.mywebsite.com) and mywebsite.com aren't the same URL? When incorporating your domain in the app, you get to designate the root domain. Subsequently, in your registrar account (where you just were), you'll need to establish a redirect so that the non-root domain steers traffic to the chosen root domain within the app.

Put simply, if you add [www.mywebsite.com](http://www.mywebsite.com) to the app, navigate to your registrar, and configure a redirect for mywebsite.com. This ensures that, regardless of which version someone enters, they'll seamlessly reach the intended destination. Check out these helpful articles to guide you through the setup:

## FAQs




**Question:** What is a domain, and why is it important for my business?
**Answer:** A domain is a unique web address that allows users to easily find your business online, such as your website or funnel. It serves as your primary online identity, helping to establish a professional and streamlined way for customers to access information about your business.




**Question:** Where can I buy a domain for my business?
**Answer:** You can purchase domains from several reputable registrars, including:

-   **CloudFlare:** Known for its security features and performance optimization.
-   **GoDaddy:** Offers a user-friendly interface and a wide selection of domain extensions.
-   **Namecheap:** Provides affordable domain registration with domain privacy options.
-   **Google Domains:** Offers transparent pricing and integration with other Google services.
-   **Bluehost:** Ideal if you’re also looking for web hosting services alongside domain registration.
-   **Hover:** Known for its straightforward domain registration process without upsells.




**Question:** What is the difference between a domain and a subdomain?
**Answer:

-   **Domain:** This is your main web address (e.g., [www.yourbusiness.com](http://www.yourbusiness.com)) and serves as your primary online identity. It operates independently and is crucial for establishing a professional presence.
-   **Subdomain:** A subdomain extends the main domain (e.g., blog.yourbusiness.com) and is useful for organizing content or representing different sections of your business, such as a blog or support page.




**Question:** Should I use a www or non-www domain?
**Answer:** Both [www.mywebsite.com](http://www.mywebsite.com) and mywebsite.com are valid URLs, but they are not the same. When setting up your domain, you will need to designate a root domain (either with or without www) and set up a redirect in your domain registrar account to ensure that traffic is directed to your chosen root domain, no matter how it is entered by the user.




**Question:** How do I set up a redirect between www and non-www domains?
**Answer:** To set up a redirect between the www and non-www versions of your domain, follow the instructions provided by your domain registrar. Here are some guides:

-   **Cloudflare:** [Redirect the www to non-www](https://community.cloudflare.com/t/redirect-the-www-to-non-www/558655)
-   **Google Domains:** [Redirecting a domain](https://support.google.com/a/answer/2518373?hl=en)This ensures that whether users type in the www version or the non-www version, they will be redirected to your preferred domain.




**Question:** When should I use subdomains for my business?
**Answer:** Subdomains are useful when you want to organize your website content into different sections or functionalities, such as having a blog (blog.yourbusiness.com) or a support page (support.yourbusiness.com). Use subdomains when you need to clearly separate different parts of your business under one main domain.




**Question:** What factors should I consider when choosing a domain registrar?
**Answer:** When selecting a domain registrar, consider factors such as pricing, customer support, domain privacy options, ease of use, and any additional services like web hosting or enhanced security features.




**Question:** Can I have both www and non-www versions of my domain active?
**Answer:** Technically, both can exist, but it’s best practice to choose one as your root domain and redirect the other to it. This helps avoid confusion and ensures consistency in your web traffic.


**Question:  Can I use multiple subdomains for different functions within my business?
**Answer:** Yes, you can use multiple subdomains to organize different parts of your business or website. For example, you could have a blog at blog.yourbusiness.com, a support section at support.yourbusiness.com, and an online store at shop.yourbusiness.com. Subdomains help in separating distinct functions while still being part of the same primary domain, which can improve both organizational structure and user experience.




**Question:** What’s the most common mistake when setting up a domain?
**Answer:** The most common issue is not setting up proper redirects between www and non-www versions or pointing the domain to the wrong destination.




**Question:** Why are domains called "indispensable assets" for my business?

**Answer:** Beyond just being a web address, a domain simplifies how customers find you. Once set up, it works automatically to connect users to your website or funnel without any recurring effort on your part, making it the foundation of your professional online presence.




**Question:** Why does the system treatwww.mywebsite.comandmywebsite.comdifferently?
**Answer:** Technically, these are two separate URLs. When you add one to your account as the "root domain," the system expects traffic to arrive via that specific path. Without a redirect at the registrar level, a user typing the "other" version might see an error page instead of your website.




**Question:** Can I use the same domain for both a website and a funnel?

**Answer:** A single specific URL (like www.yourbusiness.com) can only point to one destination at a time. However, you can use **subdomains** to handle multiple functions. For example, you could use www.yourbusiness.com for your main website and offers.yourbusiness.com for a specific lead-generation funnel.
