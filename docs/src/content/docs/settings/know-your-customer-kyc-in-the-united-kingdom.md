**Complete your UK KYC registration by linking your UK phone numbers to an approved regulatory bundle before the 31st December 2024 deadline.**

### **E-KYC Requirements for UK Phone Numbers**

### **New Regulations**

To comply with new regulations, you will need to complete the KYC process to buy new UK-based phone numbers.

### **Process Overview**

1.  New UK Numbers: KYC is required for any new UK phone number purchases made after May 27, 2024.
2.  Existing UK Numbers: For existing UK phone numbers, KYC must be completed by September 30, 2024.

### **FAQs:**




**Question:** Are there any fees for registering UK long codes?Answer**: No, there are no fees associated with registering UK long codes.




**Question:** I'm an individual customer and don’t have the required identity or address documentation. What should I do?Answer**: Unfortunately, you won’t be able to use ’s UK long codes without the necessary documentation.




**Question:** I'm a business customer, but don’t have the required company documents. What should I do?Answer**: If your business is registered with the UK House Register, you won’t need to upload additional documentation—just provide the required information for verification. If your business isn’t registered in the UK, please contact us for guidance.




**Question:** What is meant by an emergency address?Answer**: An emergency address is the physical location of the actual user of the phone number, where emergency services can dispatch help if needed.




**Question:** My business isn’t registered in the UK. Which long codes can I use?Answer**: If your business isn’t UK-registered, you should use UK Mobile or UK Toll-Free numbers instead of UK long codes.




**Question:** What should I do if my business isn't registered?Answer**: We recommend registering your business to ensure smooth operation.




**Question:** I have multiple RC bundles and phone numbers. How should I assign numbers to RC bundles?Answer**: Avoid duplicate RC bundles. Each RC bundle should have unique details, and each phone number should be assigned to a bundle that corresponds to its use, such as assigning a London office number to the London-specific RC bundle.




**Question:** What happens to my messaging or voice solutions if I don’t submit my KYC information?Answer**: Starting May 27, 2024, customers will not be able to purchase new UK long codes without an approved UK RC bundle. Existing numbers purchased before May 27, 2024, without a KYC bundle by September 30, 2024, will have all traffic blocked.




**Question:** Are the Ofcom regulatory guidelines available to the public?Answer**: Yes, Ofcom's regulatory guidelines were released to the public on 15 November 2022. You can access them by clicking on this [**_link_**](https://www.ofcom.org.uk/siteassets/resources/documents/consultations/category-2-6-weeks/232890-good-practice-guide-on-sub-allocated-and-assigned-numbers/associated-documents/annex2-good-practice-guide.pdf?v=328768)_._




**Question:** What is the UK KYC Registration Reminder for sub-accounts?Answer**: To help sub-accounts comply with the UK KYC registration process, a banner alert will appear for unverified UK phone numbers in your account. This reminder encourages completing registration by linking UK numbers to an approved regulatory bundle before the 31st December 2024 deadline.  




**Question:** I am a business customer registering with a UK CRN. Do I still need to submit Proof of Identity?
**Answer**: No. If you are a business and select UK CRN under the Registration Authority, you are only required to submit Proof of Address and an Emergency Address. Proof of Identity is no longer required for this specific registration type.  




**Question:** How will I know if my documents have been accepted?

**Answer:** After submission, the system will display a status update on the Regulatory Bundle page, showing whether your verification is pending, approved, or rejected.  




**Question:** What is the significance of the December 31, 2024, deadline compared to the earlier September 30 deadline?
**Answer:** While existing UK numbers had a KYC completion deadline of September 30, 2024, the **December 31, 2024** date is the final absolute deadline to link those numbers to an _approved_ regulatory bundle. Failure to meet this end-of-year deadline will result in SMS sent from international long codes to the UK being blocked to comply with updated UK messaging regulations.
