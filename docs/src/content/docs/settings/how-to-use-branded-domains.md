Branded Domains address the need for enhanced customization and control over outbound communications. By allowing users to add unique brand domains at the account level, ensures that each client interaction reflects the individual identity of their business, thereby safeguarding deliverability and improving overall communication effectiveness.

## **Understanding the Importance of Branded Domains:**

Previously, users faced challenges with using shared domains across all accounts, which could potentially impact email deliverability and brand reputation. With Branded Domains, each account can now utilize its unique domain, isolating the reputation and ensuring a tailored experience for clients.

## FAQs




**Question:** What are Branded Domains, and why are they important?
**Answer:** Branded Domains allow users to add unique brand domains at the account level, enhancing customization and control over outbound communications. This ensures that each client interaction reflects the individual identity of the business, safeguards email deliverability, and improves overall communication effectiveness by isolating the reputation of each account.




**Question:** How do Branded Domains improve email deliverability and brand reputation?
**Answer:** By using Branded Domains, each account can utilize its own unique domain rather than sharing a domain across multiple accounts. This isolation helps protect the domain's reputation and improves email deliverability, as emails are sent from a domain that is specifically associated with the sender’s brand.




**Question:** How do I set up a Branded Domain in ?
**Answer:** To set up a Branded Domain:

-   **Step 1:** Create a CNAME record at your DNS provider, pointing to "brand.ludicrous.cloud". This step is necessary for domain verification and authentication.
-   **Step 2:** Navigate to Account Settings in the platform and access the Company section. Add your branded domain in the input field, ensuring you include the prefix "link." before your desired domain.




**Question:** What is the process for authorizing a Branded Domain?
**Answer:** There are two ways to authorize a Branded Domain:

-   **Automatic Setup:** If you use a supported domain provider like GoDaddy, will automatically create and authorize the necessary DNS records for your branded domain.
-   **Manual Setup:** For other domain providers like Cloudflare or Squarespace, you will need to manually add a CNAME record pointing to "brand.ludicrous.cloud" in your DNS settings.




**Question:** How do I verify a Branded Domain after setting it up?
**Answer:** To verify a Branded Domain:

-   **Automatic Verification:** will automatically verify and connect your branded domain upon authorization.
-   **Manual Verification:** If you added the CNAME record manually, check your DNS settings to ensure the record is correctly configured and connected.




**Question:** What changes will I see once the Branded Domain is successfully connected?
**Answer:** Once the Branded Domain is successfully connected, you will notice that all outbound communications within the account, such as invoices, forms, and calendar links, will feature your branded domain. This provides a cohesive and professional experience for your clients, reinforcing your brand identity across all communication channels.




**Question:** What should I do if my domain provider is not supported for automatic setup?
**Answer:** If your domain provider is not supported for automatic setup, you will need to manually add the CNAME record to your DNS settings. This involves creating a new CNAME record pointing to "brand.ludicrous.cloud" and then verifying the setup by checking your DNS settings.




**Question:** Can I use any domain provider to set up a Branded Domain?
**Answer:** Yes, you can use any domain provider to set up a Branded Domain, but the process may differ depending on whether your provider supports automatic setup. For unsupported providers, you will need to manually add and verify the CNAME record in your DNS settings.




**Question:** What is the purpose of including the prefix "link." before my branded domain?
**Answer:** Including the prefix "link." before your branded domain helps ensure proper configuration and functionality of the Branded Domain across all communication channels. This prefix is a standard practice that aligns with domain settings and authentication requirements.




**Question:** How does setting up a Branded Domain affect my existing communications?
**Answer:** Setting up a Branded Domain will transform all existing outbound communications within the account, such as emails, invoices, forms, and calendar links, to feature your branded domain. This change enhances the professional appearance of your communications and reinforces your brand identity.  




**Question:** Does adding a Branded Domain improve my sender reputation automatically?
**Answer:** It doesn’t automatically improve reputation, but it **prevents your deliverability from being affected** by other users on shared domains. Over time, consistent use of your own branded domain strengthens your email reputation and credibility with ISPs




**Question:** What should I do if my manual DNS setup doesn’t work?
**Answer:** Double-check that your CNAME record points to brand.ludicrous.cloud exactly, including the link. prefix, and ensure there are no typos or extra characters. Contact support if issues persist.  




**Question:** What specific types of documents or tools will display my branded domain once it is connected?

**Answer:** Once connected, the branded domain will appear across all communication channels within your account, including **invoices, forms, and calendar links**, ensuring a cohesive brand experience for your clients.




**Question:** Does a Branded Domain affect the security (SSL) of my links?

**Answer:** Yes. Once your CNAME record is correctly pointed to brand.ludicrous.cloud, our system automatically provides an SSL certificate for your branded domain. This ensures that when a client clicks a link or opens an invoice, they see the "Secure" padlock icon in their browser.




**Question:** What is the "Step 4" verification check?

**Answer:** Step 4 is a visual review of the end result. Once connected, you should test your outbound links (like a calendar or form) to confirm they no longer use the generic platform URL and instead feature your **"\[suspicious link removed\]"** structure.
