Embedding YouTube Shorts in your email campaigns using the video element enables you to add vibrant, short video content to your emails, capturing your audience's attention more effectively and making your content more engaging and dynamic. Here’s a detailed article on how you can do that.

## How to Embed YouTube Shorts in Email Campaigns

### Step 1: Access the Email Marketing Section

**1- Navigate to Email Marketing**: Open the email marketing section **(go to the marketing tab and then select the emails tab)**  where you manage your campaigns and templates.

### Step 2: Create or Edit Your Campaign/Template

**1- Create a New Campaign**: If you're starting fresh, create a new email campaign.

**2- Edit an Existing Template**: Alternatively, select an existing template that you wish to modify.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764339235989-1.jpg)

### Step 3: Add the Video Element

**1- Drag and Drop the Video Element**: In your email editor, drag the video element into the desired location within your email layout.

### Step 4: Configure the YouTube Video

**1- Select YouTube as the Source**: Click on the video element and choose YouTube as the video source.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764339292241-2.jpg)

**2- Insert the YouTube Shorts URL**: Paste the URL of the YouTube Shorts video you want to embed.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764339368301-3.jpg)

### Step 5: Customize the Thumbnail

**1- Set the Thumbnail**: Choose a thumbnail image that complements the overall design of your email. This ensures that the video looks appealing and enticing to your recipients.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764339415259-4.jpg)

### Step 6: Preview and Save

**1- Preview Your Email**: Before finalizing, preview the email to ensure that the YouTube Shorts video is embedded correctly and that the thumbnail appears as intended.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1764339447100-5.png)

**Save Your Changes**: Once satisfied with the preview, save the template.

### FAQ’s  




**Question:** Can I embed any YouTube video, or only YouTube Shorts?
**Answer:** While YouTube Shorts are optimized for email campaigns, you can generally embed any YouTube video by following similar steps. However, keep in mind that Shorts are designed to be more engaging due to their shorter, punchy nature.




**Question:** Will the embedded video play directly in the email?
**Answer:** No, the embedded video will be displayed as a thumbnail in the email. Clicking on it will open the video on YouTube or a linked landing page where it can be played.




**Question:** Can I change the thumbnail of the YouTube Shorts video?
**Answer:** Yes, you can customize the thumbnail for the embedded video to match your email’s design and ensure it catches the reader’s attention.




**Question:** What should I do if the video doesn’t display correctly in the email?
**Answer:** Ensure that you’ve correctly pasted the YouTube Shorts URL in the video element. Additionally, check the email preview to ensure compatibility with various email clients, as some may have limitations in video support.




**Question:** How can I track the performance of embedded YouTube Shorts in my email campaign?
**Answer:** You can track the clicks and engagement of the embedded video by using UTM parameters in the YouTube link or by analyzing the performance metrics provided by your email marketing platform.




**Question:** Can I combine YouTube Shorts with other interactive elements?
**Answer:** Yes. You can include interactive elements like quizzes, surveys, or call-to-action buttons alongside the embedded video to increase engagement.  




**Question:** How do I ensure my YouTube Shorts video is working correctly before sending the email?
**Answer:** You should use the "Preview" feature within the email editor. This allows you to verify that the video is embedded correctly and that the chosen thumbnail appears as intended before finalizing your campaign.




**Question:** Do I need to create a brand-new email to use this feature?

**Answer:** Not necessarily. You have the choice to either **Create a New Campaign** from scratch or **Edit an Existing Template** that you have previously saved in your library.




**Question:** Can I use YouTube Shorts as a background for a section?

**Answer:** No. Video elements must be placed as their own content blocks. To achieve a video-background effect, you would typically need to use an animated GIF, as major email clients do not support video backgrounds.




**Question:** Will the video count as a "View" on my YouTube channel?

**Answer:** Yes. When a recipient clicks the thumbnail in your email and is redirected to YouTube to watch the Short, it counts as a legitimate view, helping to boost your video's algorithm performance on YouTube itself.
