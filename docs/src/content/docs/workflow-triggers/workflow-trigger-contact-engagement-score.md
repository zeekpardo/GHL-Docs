**Workflow trigger: Contact Engagement Score**

A Contact Engagement Score Trigger is a handy tool that starts actions in a workflow when a contact's Engagement Score changes. It helps users keep track of how engaged their contacts are, allowing for timely and relevant communication.

To determine the score, users often monitor various activities between the contact and the organization. These activities can involve actions like reading emails, scheduling appointments, completing forms, and others. Each type of activity can be assigned a different level of importance based on its relevance.

**Benefits** 

**Automates Lead Prioritization**

-   Helps businesses identify highly engaged contacts based on their interactions.
-   Automatically assigns high-value leads to sales teams for faster follow-ups.

**Targeted Marketing Campaigns**

-   Segment contacts based on engagement levels, allowing for personalized email and SMS campaigns.
-   Example: Contacts with a high engagement score can receive exclusive offers, while low-engagement contacts can get re-engagement campaigns.

**Improves Sales Efficiency**

-   Sales teams can focus on warm leads by setting automation triggers based on high engagement scores.
-   Reduces time wasted on cold leads who haven’t interacted recently.

**Increases Customer Retention & Re-Engagement**

-   Automatically detects declining engagement and triggers follow-up actions.
-   Example: If a customer hasn’t interacted in 30 days, send a re-engagement email with a discount or reminder.

**Optimizes Lead Scoring Strategy**

-   Ensures contacts are properly ranked based on website visits, email opens, link clicks, and form submissions.
-   Helps refine engagement metrics to improve the accuracy of lead qualification.

### FAQ’s




**Question:** What is a Contact Engagement Score Trigger, and how does it work?  
**Answer:** A Contact Engagement Score Trigger is an automation feature that activates workflow actions based on changes in a contact’s Engagement Score. This score reflects how engaged a contact is with your communications and interactions. By setting triggers based on specific Engagement Score thresholds, you can automate responses, follow-ups, or other actions to improve and manage engagement effectively.




**Question:** What conditions can I use to configure Contact Engagement Score Triggers?  
**Answer:** You can configure triggers using the following conditions:

-   **Equal to a score**: Activates when the Engagement Score exactly matches a specified value.
-   **Not equal to a score**: Activates when the Engagement Score does not match a specified value.
-   **Greater than a score**: Activates when the Engagement Score exceeds a specified value.
-   **Greater than or equal to a score**: Activates when the Engagement Score is greater than or equal to a specified value.
-   **Less than a score**: Activates when the Engagement Score is below a specified value.
-   **Less than or equal to a score**: Activates when the Engagement Score is less than or equal to a specified value.
-   **Is empty**: Activates when the Engagement Score field is empty.
-   **Is not empty**: Activates when the Engagement Score field is not empty.

**Question:** How does this improve sales and marketing efficiency?
**Answer:** By automatically prioritizing highly engaged contacts, sales teams can focus on warm leads, and marketing can tailor campaigns to engagement levels, reducing wasted effort on low-engagement contacts.




**Question:** Can I see a contact’s current score without going into a workflow?

**Answer:** Yes. You can see the Engagement Score directly on the **Contact Details** page. You can also create a **Smart List** in your Contacts tab to filter and show everyone who has a score greater than a certain number.




**Question:** Can I set scores to "expire" or decrease over time if a contact stops interacting?

**Answer:** Yes. This is known as **Score Decay**. Within the Score Builder, you can set rules to subtract points if a contact has been inactive for a certain period (e.g., -10 points if no email is opened for 30 days). This ensures your "Hot Leads" list stays current and isn't cluttered with people who were interested six months ago but have since gone cold.
