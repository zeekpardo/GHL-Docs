---
title: Mapping a Workflow
description: How to map a Planning Center workflow to a MinistryFlow pipeline.
---

To start syncing a workflow, you need to map it to a MinistryFlow pipeline. This tells MinistryFlow which pipeline should mirror the workflow's cards.

## Prerequisites

Both [Planning Center](/getting-started/connecting-planning-center) and [MinistryFlow](/getting-started/connecting-ministryflow) must be connected.

## Steps

1. Navigate to the **Workflows** page in MinistryFlow.
2. Click the **Add** tab.
3. Search for the Planning Center workflow you want to map. Results appear as you type.
4. Optionally, click a workflow to expand it and preview its steps.
5. Click **Select Workflow** on the one you want to map.
6. A pipeline selector will appear. Choose the MinistryFlow pipeline this workflow should map to.
7. An **auto-mapping preview** will show how workflow steps will be paired with pipeline stages (matched by position order).
8. Click **Map** to create the mapping.

## Auto-mapping

When you map a workflow to a pipeline, MinistryFlow automatically pairs steps to stages by their position:

- Step 1 maps to Stage 1
- Step 2 maps to Stage 2
- And so on...

If the workflow has more steps than the pipeline has stages (or vice versa), only the overlapping positions are mapped. You can [customize the mapping](/workflow-sync/step-mapping) after creation.

## After mapping

Once mapped, the workflow appears in the **Mapped** tab. From there you can:

- Click the workflow name to expand and [edit the step mapping](/workflow-sync/step-mapping)
- See the sync status and event count
- Delete the mapping if it is no longer needed

Changes in either system will begin syncing immediately through webhooks.
