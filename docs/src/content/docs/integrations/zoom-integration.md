Enhancing your virtual meetings has never been easier. By connecting your Zoom account to your calendar, you can generate dynamic meeting links automatically, saving time and effort. This guide will walk you through connecting your Zoom account and setting it up to work seamlessly with your calendar for efficient appointment scheduling.

## Overview of Zoom Integration

Once integrated, Zoom meeting links are generated automatically whenever a new booking is made in your calendar. This integration streamlines your workflow by eliminating manual tasks and ensures that every invite includes a unique Zoom link ready to go for your next meeting.

### Prerequisites

Before starting, ensure you have access to the Zoom account you want to connect to.

## How to Connect Your Zoom Account

**1\. Navigate to Calendar Settings:**Go to the Calendars section and click on Calendar Settings > Connections.

**2\. Locate the Video Conferencing Tab:**Find the Video Conferencing tab within the settings.

**3\. Add New Connection:**Click on "Add New" and then select "Connect" next to Zoom.  

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763742883290-Captura de pantalla 2025-11-21 113438.png>)

**4\. Sign in to Zoom:**Log in with your Zoom credentials to complete the connection.

**5\. Success Confirmation:**You’ll see a confirmation message indicating your Zoom account is now connected.

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763742904226-Captura de pantalla 2025-11-21 113459.png>)

## How to Add Zoom Links to Your Calendar

Once your Zoom integration is connected, follow these steps to add dynamic Zoom links to your calendar:

**1\. Ensure Integration is Set Up:**Confirm that your Zoom integration is successfully connected.

**2\. Edit Calendar Settings:**Go to the calendar settings for the specific calendar where you want Zoom links added.

**3\. Select the Calendar and Modify:**Open the calendar you want to update.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1773352520057-image3.png)

**4\. Choose Zoom as the Meeting Location:**Under the Staff & location, find the “Meeting Location”, and select Zoom.

**5\. Save Changes:**Click “Save” to apply the settings.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1773352547258-image4.png)

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763742989550-Captura de pantalla 2025-11-21 113622.png>)

_Note: Dynamic Zoom links are supported only for Round Robin and Collective calendars._

### Limitations with Zoom Integration

-   Each user can connect only one Zoom integration per subaccount.

## FAQs





**Question:** Which Calendars Support Dynamic Zoom Links?
**Answer:** Dynamic Zoom links are supported in Round Robin and Collective calendars only, generating a unique Zoom link for each appointment booked.




**Question:** How Can I Use Zoom with Other Calendar Types?
**Answer:** For Event & Class Booking calendars, you can manually generate a Zoom meeting link in Zoom and paste it as the meeting location in your calendar settings.

**Steps:**

-   Generate the Zoom meeting link in your Zoom account.
-   In the calendar settings, choose “Custom” as the meeting location.




**Question:** Can I Connect Multiple Zoom Accounts?
**Answer:** No, each subaccount can only connect to one Zoom account.




**Question:** Will each appointment get a unique Zoom link?
**Answer:** Yes, as long as the calendar type is **Round Robin** or **Collective**, each new booking will automatically generate a unique Zoom link.




**Question:** What happens if I change my Zoom password?
**Answer:** If you update your Zoom password, the connection to our platform will break for security reasons. You will need to go back to **Calendar Settings > Connections**, disconnect your account, and then click "Connect" again to sign in with your new password.  




**Question:** What happens if two people book a meeting at the same time?

**Answer:** Zoom generally does not allow one account to host two simultaneous meetings. If you have a "Round Robin" calendar with multiple team members, each team member should **connect their own individual Zoom account** under their profile settings to avoid scheduling conflicts.




**Question:** What happens if a customer’s card is declined?

**Answer:** If the payment fails, the appointment will **not** be booked. The system requires a successful transaction before it creates the calendar event and triggers any "Appointment Confirmation" workflows. This ensures you never have "unpaid" meetings on your schedule.
