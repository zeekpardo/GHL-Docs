With Facebook and Instagram Shops, store owners can seamlessly publish and sync their product catalogs from the E-commerce stores directly to the Meta Commerce Manager. This integration enables e-commerce businesses to extend their reach and boost sales across social platforms with ease. In addition, real-time inventory sync ensures you can manage a single inventory, run targeted ads, and connect with customers in new ways without needing separate systems.

**Key Features and Benefits**

-   **Effortless Product Syncing**: Automatically sync your products from the E-commerce store to Facebook and Instagram, ensuring an up-to-date product catalog across platforms.
-   **Streamlined Sales**: Connect with potential buyers directly on Facebook and Instagram, leveraging targeted ads to reach the right audience at the right time.
-   **Single Inventory Management**: Sync inventory in real-time so that any updates or changes to product stock reflect on both platforms simultaneously, reducing manual effort.
-   **Flexible Control Over Product Syncing**: Manage product sync options for each page and store individually, allowing for greater flexibility in managing multiple brands or product lines.

**Important Notes:**

-   **Ensure Proper Catalog Setup**: Confirm that the Facebook page has a product catalog linked to enable seamless product syncing.
-   **Category Check**: Your Facebook page should have an “E-commerce Website” category under **About** > **Contact and Basic Info** to appear correctly in the Shops section.
-   The Facebook Page should be connected to a store under the Shops section in order for the sync to take place.
-   **One-Time Product Sync**: Only non-recurring (one-time) products are compatible with Meta Marketplace.
-   **Manage Inventory Carefully**: Disabling “track inventory” or selecting “continue selling out of stock” sets the inventory quantity to 999 by default, as Meta Commerce Manager requires an inventory count.

### **FAQs**




**Question:** Can I link multiple Facebook pages to one store?
**Answer:** Yes, the integration supports multiple Facebook pages.




**Question:** How do I handle recurring products?
**Answer:** Currently, only one-time products can be synced with Meta Marketplace; recurring products are not supported.




**Question:** What if my Facebook page doesn’t have an “E-commerce Website” category?
**Answer:** You can update this by visiting your Facebook Page’s **About** section and adding “E-commerce Website” under **Contact and Basic Info**.




**Question:** Why does my product inventory show as 999 in Meta Commerce Manager?  
**Answer:** This occurs when “track inventory” is disabled or “continue selling out of stock” is enabled. Meta requires a defined inventory, so it defaults to 999.




**Question:** What if I need to disable product sync temporarily?  
**Answer:** You can easily enable or disable product sync for individual pages in the **Shops** tab, allowing you to control which products appear on Facebook and Instagram.




**Question:** How do I reconnect the integration if I’ve connected it before?
**Answer:** To reconnect, disconnect and reconnect the integration. Remove the integration from Facebook (Settings > Privacy > Business Integrations), then reconnect to enable the Shops feature and ensure proper permissions for catalog management.




**Question:** What should I do if I encounter an error saying "Catalog Management is disabled"?
**Answer:** If you receive this error, it means that the necessary permissions for catalog management are not enabled. To resolve this, ensure that you have granted the required permissions during the connection process. Reconnect the integration, remove the LeadConnector integration on Facebook’s side, and make sure all necessary permissions are enabled for catalog management.  




**Question:** What happens to a product on Instagram if it goes out of stock in my store?

**Answer:** If you have "Track Inventory" enabled, the system will sync the "0" quantity to Meta. Depending on your Meta settings, the product will either show as "Out of Stock" or be hidden from the shop until you replenish your inventory.  




**Question:** If I am creating a new Facebook Page for my shop, is there a specific category I must select for the integration to work?
**Answer:** Yes. When creating a Facebook Page, you **must** select "E-commerce Website" as the category. If you have an existing page with a different category, you must update it in the "About" section under "Contact and Basic Info" to ensure the integration functions correctly and the page appears in the Shops section.
