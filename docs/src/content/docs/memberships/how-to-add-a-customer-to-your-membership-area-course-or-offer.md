It’s important to understand the different options for how you add somebody to your product, to your offer, and to your membership area as a whole. Follow along to learn how you can use workflows and funnels to do so.

## Part 1: Granting Access Using a Funnel

### Step 1: How to Give Access via a Funnel

Granting access to a membership area requires you to create a payment product that links to your membership offer.

**Important:** Ensure that you have an offer created, if not then simply navigate to **Memberships** on the left > **Courses** at the top >From the dropdown select **Offers** > Save Offer and fill in the details for the title, products, and pricing.

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763141082265-Captura de pantalla 2025-11-14 122433.png>)

**Please note that the offer will remain in the "DRAFT" tab until published**

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763141492268-Captura de pantalla 2025-11-14 123121.png>)

That offer links to your membership product as an included course when you create an offer.

-   Go to the products menu in Payments >Products and select Create Product

-   Fill in the data requested, and select “additional options” under the pricing section to toggle on and select your membership offer.

**Please note that the membership offer must be published for it to appear in the dropdown.**

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXd2Oq4oGbtvWODE9iUNMct6NjVtYNgjr0V_qn_oCbBQz87WveBLcgcJr1rlkBRvRhEL5bDHKdRx6mwy49ugCvvovV8oxsxxfdnWilBq-JIxlPB0fcJDhcIUptDnaQPhxPBGJiGzsBwCxFPT96vWLZ-hSvnn?key=zlYkvUAhVuYni7sm0lBjew)

**Please note that the currency will be determined according to the location of the sub-account**

-   If you need to make an existing product a membership offer click on the product and edit the price. Then toggle on the membership offer and save

### Step 2: Giving the customer access to the membership area in the same funnel after they make their purchase.

-   Navigate back to the funnel overview and select or create a page/step after the purchase of the membership.
-   Click Edit.
-   In the editor, add a new button to the funnel. Then scroll down the button settings.
-   Click on the button actions and change it to link to “Membership”. This enables the button so customers will be directed to the membership area upon clicking.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXdkTcWhrsNz8cymWZZ1hd5QMrJBymfKCl1-3fxC8H8ptHNza7qZcinIzY2zM6WhKzbY8OgtEJrWaAuuXa1Q3W1kGWlD5Xf7O5Geo9Puj_cOnAFOgjFIw_P4SLu9CWtgDVwwJBKym3PzAg7cDH3li2MDOwDp?key=zlYkvUAhVuYni7sm0lBjew)

**_Note:_** _It is common for customers to be sent their log in information through a separate email once they’ve purchased a membership product. However, if you want them to get direct access right away, this option works too. (Check out our other tutorials on how to create and send welcome emails with login details.)_

**_Note:_** _You can also use a magic link to accomplish this with a URL see our article on that for more information_

### Option 2: Sending credential emails using Workflows

-   Add the Membership new Signup trigger to the second workflow

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1773843626041-image12.png)

**Note:** You can select an offer if you want to trigger the workflow for any specific offer. 

-   Add the Send Email action and either use a template that includes Course custom values or build one within the workflow email builder.
-   Under the email action, click on the custom value option > Course > New Signup and select the values ‘Email’ and ‘Login URL(MAGIC LINK)
    -   Email: The email custom value will populate the contact’s email with which they can log in to the course.
    -   Login URL: The login URL will send them a link where they can set their login password.

**_Please Note: New Signup_** _signifies an action was done by the contact – they filled out a form, made a purchase, etc., or can be viewed as the first offer granted._ **_Offer Access Granted_** _implies an action done by YOU or a workflow giving them permission to access the offer after the first time._

**FAQ:**




**Question:** What happens if I don’t publish my membership offer?
**Answer:** If you don't publish your membership offer, it will remain in the "DRAFT" tab, and it won’t be available for selection in your products or funnels. Make sure to publish the offer for it to be active and selectable.




**Question:** How is the currency for the offer determined?

**Answer:** The currency for the offer is automatically determined based on the location of the .




**Question:** Do I need to do anything after the payment is made to grant membership access?

**Answer:** No additional setup is required after the payment is made. The user will be automatically added to the membership course once the payment is processed.




**Question:** Why aren’t login credentials being sent to my customers?

**Answer:** If credentials aren’t being sent, ensure that the "Send Welcome Email" option is toggled on in the Membership settings. Alternatively, check the workflow to ensure the appropriate triggers and actions are set up correctly.




**Question:** Can I manually grant access to a membership offer?

**Answer:** Yes, you can manually grant access from the contact page, but it’s not recommended if you’re using a workflow to send login credentials. This method may not work correctly with templates set up in Membership settings.




**Question:** Can I revoke access later if needed?

**Answer:** Yes. You can remove access manually from the contact profile or use workflows with revocation actions based on conditions such as refunds, cancellations, or expired access.




**Question:** What is the difference between the "New Signup" and "Offer Access Granted" triggers in a workflow?
**Answer:** "New Signup" refers to an action taken by the contact, such as completing a purchase or filling out a form for the first time. "Offer Access Granted" refers to an action taken by the user (you) or a workflow that grants permission to access an offer after the initial signup.  




**Question:** Can I use an "Order Bump" on both Funnels and Websites?
**Answer:** Yes. An Order Bump is a great way to offer a small "add-on" product (like an ebook or a workbook) directly on the checkout page. You can customize the look and feel of the bump box within the element settings.
