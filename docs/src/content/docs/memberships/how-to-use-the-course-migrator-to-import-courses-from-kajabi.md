In this article, we'll guide you through the process of utilizing the course migrator to import your courses from your Kajabi account. With the Kajabi Course Migrator feature, you can effortlessly transfer valuable content to the platform. The import Wizard now allows you to import videos and not only text and images. Moreover, you will be able to see the status of the importing process. 

**Best Practices:**

-   To enhance your experience, it's advised to avoid using custom CSS.
-   Make sure to back up your themes. This precaution will allow you to restore your original settings if necessary.
-   Schedule the migration during off-hours to reduce any potential disruption to your learners' experience with the course.

You can follow the steps below to import your course from Kajabi.

## **Navigation**

### **Step 1: Navigate to the Membership Tab**

Firstly, log in to your platform and navigate to the membership section on the left. From the Courses tab dropdown select products.

### **Step 2: Create a New Course**

Click on '+ Create Product' to initiate the creation of a new course. This step prepares the framework for importing your content.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763751138811-image.png)

### **Step 3: Import from Kajabi**

Look for the option to import from Kajabi. Once located, click on it, and a dialogue box will appear prompting you to enter your Kajabi email address, password, and domain. Follow the next steps to get the details to create the new contact based on the email provided (#1) with the password (#2) and also to get the domain to enter in the course importer (#3).  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1769741640900-image3.png)

### **Step 4: Locate Your Course in Kajabi**

Switch to your Kajabi account and find the course you wish to migrate to. Take note of all the modules, lessons, and quizzes included in that course.

### **Step 5: Import Process:**

-   Once the import process begins, you'll be logged in using your learner credentials, and the courses will start importing.

## **Important Considerations Before Importing:**

**Import options:** The import will only bring in lessons that have been published. Additionally, the assignments, quizzes, and assessments will not be included in the import process.

**Image Compatibility:** Images embedded within text content in lesson materials may not be migrated. You might need to re-upload them if needed.

**Theme Requirements:** Ensure that the theme used for your course in Kajabi is a Premier theme. If not, customize it accordingly. Similarly, the theme used for the library page should be the Encore theme. Adjust as necessary.

### **Step 5: Capture Your Kajabi Domain**

You'll need to capture your Kajabi domain to initiate the import process. This can be found in the settings section under site details. Your domain name will typically include ".kajabi" at the end.

An alternative method to obtain your domain name is by **adding a contact** as a learner or student. By viewing the contact details, you'll find the domain name embedded in the link sent to that contact.

Ensure you create a **new contact** within the platform you are importing from. You can either use an email with the client club domain or any email address to create contacts. Make sure you are granting the offers as well.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXdSYLe0YWvQGfdCcvlh3lT195w25-IGlFVk5-NABr_7EUW2iWDIvpYg2QwiyiHrKm3umKj57M5ikY4rqYeOAoGqYLZQcUaeYkRG1jaZStG08BfSlyh6nG6QffhRBFW6-BSuEBoC-L3xOCmRV_jrLaGSKn_R?key=m18VkCPMewLFtxpkd8Rq3g)

-   Once created, go back to the Course Importer screen and enter the password you used to create the account in the previous step.
-   Add the learner domain in the domain field (the domain where you enter learner login credentials). This domain is found in Site details in Kajabi. Make sure you add https:// at the beginning of the domain and “login/” at the end of it. 

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1769741763109-image2.png)

-   Click the "Import" button

### **Step 6: Initiate Import**

Return to your new platform and enter your Kajabi domain (including https://) into the designated field. Click Import to commence the migration process. You should see a green status notice on the top right corner of the import wizard.   

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1769741796190-image7.png)

### **Step 7: Monitor Import Status**

-   Progress will be displayed, but be sure to click the "Refresh" button to view the most accurate status.
-   To stop the import process, simply click the "Cancel" button.
-   You can switch tabs while the import is in progress, but do **NOT** close the tab or window.
-   All videos, images, and text content from the courses (and lessons) will be migrated.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXcBJjpS7WbCJ45jOtqVN5-hLP1YgU9hN4xkvwVozRXn7YU_uXvkFMvJ15WK8O0b83PBdU6OwAmN8R-Cfx3AhdPSpQhEFopYqhfT9PkDie25dnTR9uzm7wjFHRXWsRyXaRxVdP6t?key=m18VkCPMewLFtxpkd8Rq3g)

**_Note:_**_Kajabi Course Migrator extends its functionality to include the copying of videos in addition to text and images._

-   Products that get migrated will be in a processing status until all their associated videos are successfully copied.
-   Upon the completion of video copying, the status will be updated to complete

### **Additional Features**

-   **Email Domain Flexibility for Course Imports:** Users can use any email address to create contacts, removing previous domain restrictions.
-   **Consent Form for Course Imports:** A consent form is added for the course import process. Users must confirm that they own the intellectual property of the imported course. This ensures compliance with intellectual property laws.
-   **File Support:** Lessons support all types of file extensions as attachments.

## FAQs




**Question:** What causes an authentication error?

**Answer:** Authentication errors appear when the added domain or credentials are incorrect. To resolve this, carefully verify the credentials again and ensure they are correct before initiating the import process.

**Question:** What causes a "course import failed" error?

**Answer:** Possible reasons for import failure:  
**Incorrect Theme:** Ensure that the selected theme is correct.  
**Credential Verification:** Double-check the accuracy of the provided credentials.  
**Valid Domain:** Confirm that the provided domain is compatible with the "/login" path. For example, "domainName/login" should lead to the login page.  
**Retry Option:** If the import fails, users can click on "retry" to resume the process from where it left off.  
**Cancellation Clarification:** If a user cancels the import, it will be considered as canceled by the user, and any new imports will start from the beginning.

Question: How do I obtain my Kajabi domain for the import?

**Answer:** Your Kajabi domain can be found in your Kajabi settings under Site Details. It typically ends with “.kajabi.” You can also get it by adding a contact as a learner and viewing the domain link sent to that contact.

Question: Is there a consent requirement for course imports?  
**Answer:** Yes, users must confirm that they own the intellectual property of the course during the import process to ensure compliance with copyright laws.

Question: Can I switch tabs or work on the platform while the import is in progress?  
**Answer:** Yes, you can switch tabs, but do not close the browser tab or window where the import is running, as this may interrupt the process.

Question: Can I import the same course twice if I update it in Kajabi?  
**Answer:** Yes, but it will create a new product in your library. It won't "update" the one you imported previously. If you've made major changes in Kajabi, it’s often easier to delete the old import and run a fresh one.

Question: What are the theme requirements for a successful migration?  

**Answer:** For the migrator to function correctly, the course in Kajabi should use a **Premier theme**, and the library page should use the **Encore theme**.If your Kajabi site uses different themes, you should customize them to these requirements before starting.
