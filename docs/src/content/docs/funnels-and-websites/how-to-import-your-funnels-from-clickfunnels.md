Importing your funnel pages from ClickFunnels can save you time and effort, eliminating the need to rebuild pages from scratch when migrating to . This article will guide you through the steps for importing both ClickFunnels Classic and the ClickFunnels 2.0 pages.

### **Key Features & Benefits**

-   **ClickFunnels 2.0 Import Support:** Alongside existing support for ClickFunnels Classic, the ClickFunnels 2.0 Importer allows you to migrate pages with ease.
-   **Supported Elements**: The importer currently includes key elements such as:

-   **Text Elements**: Headline, Sub-Headline, Paragraph, Bullet List.
-   **Media Elements**: Image and Video.
-   **Interactive Elements**: Buttons.

-   Enhanced Flexibility: Enjoy a wider range of options for building and optimizing your funnel within CompanyName.

**Note:Unsupported Elements**: Certain elements are not yet supported including:

-   **Layout Elements**: Flex Section and Row.
-   **Interactive and Specialized Elements**: Icon, Video Popup, Audio, Blogs, Posts, Countdown Timer, Checkout, Nav Menu, and Progress Bar.

### **Step 1: Grabbing the Link from ClickFunnels**

-   Navigate to your ClickFunnels account and grab the funnel step URL you wish to import.
-   Copy the link (be sure it’s the https:// version)

### **Step 2: Importing the Funnel**

1.  **Navigate to Your Site Management Area:**
    -   Go to **Sites** in your dashboard.
    -   Click on **Funnels**.
2.  **Create a New Funnel:**
    -   Click on **Create a New Funnel**.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXehi-FnqbSVL2L_cj1A6kNHvIwT47SjS8pKeripNaIbi_uRWuGSmEuRvsmOPMfee6d4D5gm_iSaX9kj7tTbZvPf0iy6tHJXdpehOezB_aRipKq5J64ZNEiscyAOn42kzsS9E1hfqQ?key=fPVKekOoeb9OGTFknUrp1w)

-   Give your new funnel a name to identify it easily.

1.  **Add a New Funnel Step:**
    -   Click on **Add New Step or Import**
    -   Enter the name of the page you are importing.
    -   Paste the ClickFunnels Live URL you copied earlier into the provided field.

-   This will not work with the Share ClickFunnels URL. You need to use the Live URL. 
-   You can import one ClickFunnels page at a time

### **ClickFunnels 2.0** 

For ClickFunnels 2.0, you can import many essential elements. However, please be aware of unsupported elements that may not transfer, such as **Flex Sections, Rows, Icons**, and specialized elements like **Countdown Timers** and **Checkout forms**.

### **Pro Tips**

-   **Review Imported Pages**: Once imported, review your funnel for any unsupported elements that might need manual adjustment.
-   **Use Supported Elements Efficiently**: Maximize supported elements like headlines and buttons to achieve a clean, engaging design.

### **FAQ's**




**Question:** What type of URL should I use for importing?

**Answer:** You should use the Live URL (https://) of the funnel step page. The Share URL is not supported for import.




**Question:** Can I import multiple pages at once?

**Answer:** No, the import process supports only one ClickFunnels page at a time.




**Question:** What should I do if my import fails?

**Answer:** Ensure that you are using the Live URL and not the Share URL. Double-check the URL format and try importing again. If issues persist, consult support for further assistance.




**Question:** Can I edit the imported funnel page after import?

**Answer:** Yes, once the funnel page is imported, you can edit and customize it as needed within your new platform.




**Question:** Will all my ClickFunnels page elements be preserved during the import?

**Answer:** Most elements should be preserved, but some formatting or functionality may need adjustments. Review the imported page and make any necessary edits.




**Question:** What happens if an element is unsupported?

**Answer:** Unsupported elements won’t appear after import. You can manually add similar elements in if needed.




**Question:** Can I import both Classic and 2.0 ClickFunnels pages?

**Answer:** Yes, both versions are supported.




**Question:** If I have a video on my page, will the video file be moved to my Media Library?

**Answer:** The importer will bring over the Video Element and the link (e.g., YouTube or Vimeo URL). It does not download the video file itself. Your video will still play from its original hosting source.




**Question:** Why can't I use the "Share URL" to import my funnel?

**Answer:** A Share URL is a proprietary link designed to work only within its original ecosystem. To "read" the design and structure of your page, our importer requires the **Live URL** (the actual web address where your page is visible to the public). This allows the system to accurately map the HTML and CSS elements to our builder.
