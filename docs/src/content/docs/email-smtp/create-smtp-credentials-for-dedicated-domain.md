**Create SMTP Credentials For Dedicated Domain**

The ability to generate SMTP credentials for dedicated sending domains enhances email delivery capabilities. This feature allows you to create unique SMTP credentials for your specific domain, ensuring secure and authenticated access for sending emails. It also provides integration with warm-up services, optimizing your email-sending practices and improving deliverability rates.

**SMTP and Email Limits**

**SMTP Limit:**You can create up to five SMTP credentials per dedicated domain.

**Daily Email Limit:**Each account is restricted to sending 1,500 emails per day.  
If this limit is exceeded, all SMTP credentials associated with dedicated domains will be automatically deleted.

## Where can I find my IMAP settings?

IMAP is used for retrieving messages, while SMTP is for sending data.

**Note**: As we do not host mailboxes, we do not support POP or IMAP protocols. SMTP credentials cannot be used in IMAP settings.

To find your IMAP settings, refer to your email provider’s settings.

**Example**: For Google, generate an app password in your Google account:

-   **Username**: Your email address
-   **Password**: Your app password
-   **IMAP host**: imap.gmail.com
-   **IMAP port**: 993

**Pro Tips:**

**1- Warm-Up Your Domain:** Use the SMTP credentials to connect your domain with a warm-up service. This is crucial, especially for new domains, as it helps to build a positive sender reputation and reduces the likelihood of emails landing in spam folders.

**2- Create Unique SMTP Users for Team Members:** Assign individual SMTP credentials for each team member. This not only enhances security but also allows you to track and manage email activity more effectively.

**3- Regularly Verify Domain Settings:** Periodically check that your NS settings are correct and verified in your domain service provider to ensure seamless email delivery and avoid disruptions.

**4- Use Strong,** **Unique Passwords:** When creating SMTP credentials, use strong and unique passwords. Regularly update these passwords to maintain security, especially when connecting to external services.

**5- Monitor Email Deliverability:** Keep an eye on the email deliverability metrics available in your platform. If issues arise, you can quickly adjust settings or re-verify your domain to improve performance.

### FAQ’s: 




**Question:** What is the purpose of using SMTP credentials for Email-dedicated domains?

**Answer:** SMTP credentials allow you to send emails using a dedicated domain, integrate with third-party services like warm-up tools, and manage email deliverability more effectively.




**Question:** How do I verify a new domain for SMTP use?

**Answer: To verify a new domain, add the domain in your platform, then insert the provided NS settings into your domain service provider. Once these settings are correctly configured and verified, your domain will be ready to use.




**Question:** Can I set up multiple SMTP users for the same domain?**   

**Answer:** Yes, you can create multiple SMTP users for a single domain. This is useful for assigning different email addresses to various team members or departments within your company.




**Question:** What should I do if my emails are landing in spam?**   

**Answer:** If your emails are going to spam, ensure your domain is properly warmed up using a warm-up service. Also, verify your domain settings, review your email content for spam triggers, and regularly monitor your sender's reputation.




**Question:** How can I reset the password for an SMTP user?
**Answer:** To reset an SMTP user’s password, go to the SMTP settings, select the user, click on 'Reset Password,' enter the new password twice, and click 'Reset Password' to update it.


**Question:Why is domain warm-up important and how do I do it?

**Answer:** Domain warm-up gradually builds your sender reputation by sending a controlled number of emails over time.  
You can integrate your **SMTP credentials with a warm-up service** to automate this process and improve inbox placement.


**Question:Can I use these SMTP credentials to receive emails?

**Answer:** No. SMTP credentials are only for sending emails. Since the platform does not host mailboxes, incoming email must be managed through your email provider (e.g., Google, Ionos, Bluehost).




**Question:** Can I delete SMTP credentials?

**Answer:** Yes. In SMTP settings, click the three dots next to the SMTP user, select **Delete**, and confirm to remove the credentials.




**Question:** If I delete an SMTP user, will my previous email statistics be deleted too?

**Answer:** No. Your email campaign statistics and history are stored within the platform’s database. Deleting an SMTP user only revokes the permission to send _new_ emails using those specific credentials.




**Question:** What is the daily email limit, and what happens if I exceed it?

**Answer:** Each account is restricted to sending 1,500 emails per day. If you exceed this limit, all SMTP credentials associated with your dedicated domains will be automatically deleted.
