**Dedicated IP Guide**

A dedicated IP address allows your emails to be sent from a unique, exclusive address, giving you complete control over the sender's reputation and email deliverability. Email service providers monitor these IPs to determine how reliably your emails reach their destinations.  

**When should I get a dedicated IP address?**

If you're sending large volumes of emails—typically over 200,000 per month—using a dedicated IP can be crucial for building a reliable sender reputation. This investment enhances email security protocols like SPF, DMARC, DKIM, and SSL, protecting against spoofing. Unlike shared IPs, a dedicated IP prevents risks associated with other senders' actions and safeguards against domain hijacking, ensuring secure email communication.

**Eligibility for a Dedicated IP:**

Accounts with a total contact count exceeding 100,000 or those that have sent more than 200,000 emails in the last 30 days are recommended to get a Dedicated IP.

**Benefits of Dedicated IP:Enhanced Email Delivery**: Dedicated IPs often lead to quicker email delivery times. Major email providers such as Gmail and Yahoo generally prefer emails sent from dedicated IPs, which helps ensure faster delivery directly to inboxes. Additionally, your performance remains unaffected by other users since you're not sharing bandwidth.

**Complete Control Over Sender and IP Reputation**: Owning a dedicated IP means you control your sending practices and IP reputation. This autonomy allows for tailored strategies without external disruptions.

**Streamlined Issue Resolution**: With a dedicated IP, you're the sole sender, which simplifies the process of identifying and resolving any IP-related issues. This direct approach enhances the reliability and effectiveness of your services.

**Boosted Cybersecurity**: A dedicated IP adds a protective layer against cybersecurity threats such as domain hijacking, malware, and hacking attempts, thereby securing your online activities more robustly.



**Using a Dedicated IP (Best Practices):**

In the world of email service providers (ESPs), maintaining a good reputation is crucial. When using a dedicated IP, it's important to carefully manage the volume and timing of your emails to avoid triggering spam filters. Ideally, you should gradually increase your email volume every 24 hours to ensure a smooth escalation.

Dedicated IPs prove most effective for consistently sending large volumes of emails. However, sporadic bursts of high-volume sending can backfire, leading to being marked as a spammer by email filters or even resulting in being blocklisted. ESPs are particularly sensitive to sudden spikes in email activity.

For those with irregular, high email volumes, a shared IP may be more beneficial, blending your sporadic high volumes with the consistent traffic of other users on the same IP.


In the dynamic field of email delivery, skillful strategy and tact are vital to maintaining an excellent reputation with ESPs and ensuring your messages are delivered to their intended recipients.


We recommend sending at least 50,000 messages weekly to maintain a strong reputation and minimize throttling by mailbox providers. Additionally, it's advisable to use one dedicated IP for every 1 million messages sent each month. This strategy promotes optimal deliverability and boosts your email performance overall.

### FAQ:




**Question:** When is it recommended to get a dedicated IP address for email sending?
**Answer:** A dedicated IP is recommended for accounts with a contact count exceeding 100,000 or for those sending more than 200,000 emails in the last 30 days. It's especially beneficial if you send large volumes of emails regularly, enhancing your sender reputation and email deliverability.




**Question:** How does having a dedicated IP improve email delivery?
**Answer:** A dedicated IP leads to quicker email delivery times as major providers like Gmail and Yahoo prefer emails from dedicated IPs. Since you're not sharing bandwidth with other users, your performance remains consistent, and you have full control over your sender reputation.




**Question:** What is the best practice for sending emails through a dedicated IP?
**Answer:** To maintain a strong reputation, it's recommended to gradually increase email volume daily to avoid triggering spam filters. Ideally, send at least 50,000 messages weekly and use one dedicated IP for every 1 million messages sent monthly.




**Question:** Can I assign more than one IP to a single domain?
**Answer:** Yes, you can assign multiple IPs to a single domain. If your domain is associated with more than one IP, emails will be sent through all the linked IPs in a round-robin manner, ensuring a balanced distribution of email traffic.




**Question:** How do I set up a dedicated IP in my email service?
**Answer:** Navigate to account settings, go to the Email Services tab, and select the Dedicated domain and IP under your email provider section. Click on "Buy IP," and once purchased, assign it to your domain by selecting the IP from the menu.




**Question:** What should I do if I experience delivery issues with my dedicated IP?
**Answer:** Check your sending volume and consistency. Sudden spikes can trigger spam filters. Regularly monitor your sender reputation and ensure your security protocols (SPF, DMARC, DKIM, SSL) are set up correctly.




**Question:** Is a dedicated IP suitable for sporadic email senders?
**Answer:** Not always. Sporadic high-volume sending can trigger spam filters. For irregular senders, a shared IP may be more effective since your sending is blended with other users’ traffic, reducing the risk of deliverability issues.




**Question:** If I have a Dedicated IP, does that mean my emails will never go to spam?

**Answer:** Not necessarily. A Dedicated IP gives you a "clean slate," but you still have to follow best practices. If you send content that people don't like and they click the "Report Spam" button, your Dedicated IP reputation will drop, and your emails will start landing in the spam folder.  




**Question:** Do I need to set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC if I have a Dedicated IP?

**Answer:** Absolutely. A dedicated IP provides the _pathway_, but SPF, DKIM, and DMARC provide the _identity_. Think of the IP as the car and the security protocols as the license plate; you need both to be legally recognized on the road of email delivery.




**Question:** What happens if I stop sending emails for a month?
**Answer:** Your IP reputation may "go cold." If you stop sending for more than 30 days, you should treat the IP as new and perform a mini-warm-up before returning to your peak volumes. Consistently sending at least 50,000 messages weekly is the best way to keep the IP "hot".
