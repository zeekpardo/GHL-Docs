### How does the 2-way sync work between and your email account?

There are two ways the sync can be established:

1.  You can email a contact directly from , which will start the sync between both platforms.
2.  Gmail sync works if a contact already saved in emails the user (who has Gmail two-way sync enabled) on their Gmail address. The email will sync into the conversations tab.

**Note**: Emails from contacts who are also sub-account users are not synced, as they may contain confidential information. Emails from non-user contacts will sync as described above.

### **Does Two-way sync only work with individual emails or bulk emails and workflows?**

How the sender domain mapping works for different types of emails:

-   **Individual Email**: When you connect a personal email account (like Gmail), the Gmail address will be the sender domain for emails sent individually. These emails are sent directly from Gmail when 2-way sync is enabled.

Note that Google has a daily limit of 500 emails, which applies to individual emails.

-   **Bulk Email**: Bulk emails will continue to be sent through the account's email provider. The 2-way sync does not affect this.
-   **Workflow & Automation**: Automated emails are not sent through 2-way sync but are dispatched from the account's defined email provider, ensuring smooth integration with workflows and email dispatch system.

**Please Note**:  
This setup allows users to take advantage of 2-way sync for personal emails while also being able to send bulk emails. The Gmail daily limit of 500 emails only applies to individual emails, so users can send bulk emails through the SMTP without worrying about hitting the limit, enabling seamless mass email campaigns.

### **FAQs**




**Question:** What happens to emails received from existing contacts?
**Answer:** All incoming emails from existing contacts will be synced to both your account and Gmail/Outlook inbox. This ensures you have a complete record of all communications across both platforms.




**Question:** Will removing a synced account delete existing emails?
**Answer:** No, removing a synced account does not delete existing emails. Emails previously synced will remain intact, and the sync will only affect new emails sent or received after the account is removed.




**Question:** Do new emails get synced after an account is removed?
**Answer:** New emails sent or received after removing the account will not sync between and Gmail/Outlook. The sync will continue for existing emails, but new ones won’t be included.




**Question:** Will all my existing contacts in Gmail be synced with ?
**Answer:** Yes, the Two-Way Sync will identify and sync existing contacts from Gmail to . Incoming emails from these contacts will be automatically linked to their respective records, but emails received prior to the integration cannot be synced.




**Question:** What happens when a new email is received from a contact?
**Answer:** New incoming emails from existing contacts will appear in both and Gmail. If the email is part of an existing conversation thread, it will be added to that thread on both platforms.




**Question:** What happens when you send emails from or Gmail?
**Answer:** Outgoing emails sent from will be logged in the Sent folder of the synced email account. Similarly, emails from the synced email account will appear in the corresponding contact’s conversation history.




**Question:** How are CC/BCC recipients handled in the sync?
**Answer:** If someone is CC'd or BCC'd in an email, the information will be synced to under the respective contact, but no new contact is created for those CC or BCC addresses.




**Question:** What if a contact emails someone else but includes the user?
**Answer:** The email will be reflected in under the appropriate contact, maintaining the full conversation history.




**Question:** How is the conversation thread managed when multiple recipients are involved?
**Answer:** For emails sent to multiple recipients, the first contact listed becomes the conversation tab in , with subsequent emails appearing within that thread.




**Question:** What is a BCC Address, and how does it work?
**Answer:** When sending an email from Gmail/Outlook, you can include a BCC address in the Cc or Bcc field. This automatically adds the conversation and contact to , and future emails from the contact will be synced to .




**Question:** How is the secure connection handled if Gmail login credentials change?
**Answer:** If your Gmail login credentials change, will require re-authentication and resyncing to maintain the secure connection.




**Question:** What happens when an email is forwarded from Gmail?
**Answer:** Forwarded emails to an existing contact will be treated as a sent email and synced under that contact’s record in .




**Question:** What if there are duplicate emails with multiple contacts?
**Answer:** Duplicate emails sent to multiple contacts will be associated with the first created contact in .




**Question:** How are forwarded emails handled?

**Answer:** Forwarded emails to existing contacts are treated as sent emails and synced under the contact’s record in  




**Question:** I received a "Delivery Incomplete" error after using my Auto BCC address. Is it broken?

**Answer:** Not necessarily. This is a common quirk with Gmail. Because Gmail doesn't receive a "delivery receipt" from the system's internal logging tool, it occasionally flags it. Usually, the email has still successfully logged into the contact's timeline despite the notification.




**Question:** Does the Gmail 2-way sync apply to emails sent through my automated workflows or bulk campaigns?

**Answer:** No. 2-way sync is specifically for individual emails. Bulk emails and automated workflow emails are dispatched through your account's primary SMTP/Email provider. This ensures that your personal Gmail account stays within Google’s daily limits (typically 500 emails) while allowing your automated marketing to run separately.
