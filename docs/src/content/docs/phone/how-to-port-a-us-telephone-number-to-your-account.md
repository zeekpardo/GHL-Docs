**Important Note:** 

If you transfer your personal cell phone number over to the system, that can’t be undone. It’s best to transfer existing VoIP numbers only.

**Canadian Number Porting:**

The porting process for Canadian numbers follows the same steps as US numbers. However, it typically takes between 4 to 6 weeks depending on the losing carrier. Be sure to verify the number’s capabilities post-porting to ensure expected functionality.

**Porting Fee Notice:**

There is a one-time porting fee per number for processing.

**Required Format for Porting Form:**

Numbers must be submitted in E164 format (e.g., +1XXXYYYZZZZ). Make sure the attached Letter of Authorization is dated within the last 15 days.

 **Account Number Requirement for Canadian Ports:**When porting Canadian numbers, include the customer account number as recorded with your current service provider.

**Internal Number Transfer (Project to Project):**If a number already exists within the same service provider under a different internal account, it may just need to be moved rather than ported externally. Ensure the losing account identifier is provided to facilitate the process.

### FAQ




**Question:** What information do I need to prepare for porting my number?
**Answer:** You will need your Location ID, customer name, service address, a list of telephone numbers to port, and specific details if you're porting a wireless number, such as your wireless account number and PIN/last 4 of SSN.




**Question:** Can I use a P.O. Box as my service address?
**Answer:** No, the service address must be a physical location, not a P.O. Box.




**Question:** What should the Letter of Authorization include?
**Answer:** The Letter of Authorization should include all the necessary details mentioned in the guide and be in PDF format, not exceeding 4MB in size.




**Question:** Can I port my personal cell phone number to the new system?
**Answer:** Yes, but be aware that once transferred, the process cannot be undone. It is recommended to transfer existing VoIP numbers only.




**Question:** What format should my recent phone billing statement be in?
**Answer:** The recent phone billing statement should be a scanned or electronic copy in PDF format, not exceeding 4MB in size.




**Question:** Can multiple numbers be ported at once?
**Answer:** Yes, multiple numbers can be ported together if listed properly on the Letter of Authorization and billing statement. Each number must still be in E164 format.




**Question:** Who should I send my porting documents to?
**Answer:** Send your **Letter of Authorization** and recent billing statement to the support team, who will submit the transfer request to the correct team on your behalf.




**Question:** Will my phone service be interrupted during the transfer?

**Answer:** There is usually a very brief period of "downtime" (often less than an hour) on the day the port completes as the routing switches from your old provider to our system. We recommend scheduling ports for mid-week to avoid weekend support delays.




**Question:** How long does it take to port a Canadian phone number?
**Answer:** Porting a Canadian number typically takes between 4 to 6 weeks, depending on how quickly the losing carrier responds to the request.




**Question:** Can I cancel a porting request once it has been submitted?

**Answer:** You can request a cancellation, but it is **not guaranteed**. Once the losing carrier has "released" the number to the new network, it is often impossible to stop the transfer. Always be 100% sure you want to move the number before submitting your documents.
