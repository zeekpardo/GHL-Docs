For extra fraud protection, 3D Secure (3DS) requires customers to complete an additional verification step with the card issuer when paying. Typically, you direct the customer to an authentication page on their bank’s website, and they enter a password associated with the card or a code sent to their phone. This process is familiar to customers through the card networks’ brand names, such as Visa Secure and Mastercard Identity Check.

The Strong Customer Authentication regulation in Europe requires the use of 3DS for card payments.

**Note**

While 3D Secure greatly enhances payment security, it is important to recognize that no system can completely eliminate fraud. To provide a comprehensive approach to online payment security, 3DS should be used in conjunction with other security measures, such as secure payment gateways and encryption protocols.

### FAQ's


**Question:What is 3D Secure**?  **

**Answer:** 3D Secure is an additional layer of authentication for online credit card transactions. It requires cardholders to verify their identity through a one-time password or other authentication method before the transaction is completed.


**Question:How does 3D Secure reduce fraud?

**Answer:** By requiring additional verification from the cardholder, 3DS helps to ensure that the transaction is authorized by the legitimate cardholder, thus reducing the likelihood of fraudulent transactions.


**Question:Do all payment gateways support 3D Secure?

**Answer:** Most modern payment gateways support 3DS. Check with your payment service provider to ensure that your gateway is compatible and properly configured.


**Question:Is 3D Secure mandatory for all transactions?

**Answer:** 3D Secure is typically required for transactions in certain regions or under specific conditions, such as high-value purchases. However, the requirements may vary based on local regulations and payment service provider policies.




**Question:** What is Strong Customer Authentication (SCA)?

**Answer:** SCA is a European regulation that requires online payments to include multi-factor authentication, something the customer knows (e.g., password), has (e.g., phone), or is (e.g., fingerprint) to prevent fraud. 3D Secure fulfills these requirements.




**Question:** How do I enable 3D Secure for my online store?

**Answer:** You need to ensure your payment gateway supports 3DS and configure it according to the gateway’s instructions. Customers will then be prompted for authentication during checkout.




**Question:** Will 3D Secure make my checkout process take longer?

**Answer:** It adds one extra step, but usually only for a few seconds. If a bank requires it, a small window will pop up for the customer to enter a code or approve the charge in their banking app. While it adds a step, it greatly increases the customer's trust that their data is safe.  




**Question:** Does 3D Secure work with Apple Pay and Google Pay?
**Answer:** Yes. Apple Pay and Google Pay have 3DS-equivalent security built-in. Because the user has already authenticated with their face ID, fingerprint, or passcode on their device, they often don't need to do a "second" 3DS pop-up; the security requirement is met instantly.
