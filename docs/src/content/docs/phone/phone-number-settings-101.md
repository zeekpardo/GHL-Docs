**Phone Number Settings 101**

Are you unsure about the intricacies of phone number settings within ? Don't worry, mastering these settings isn't as daunting as it may seem. In this guide, we'll explore the various phone number configurations available within , ensuring you have complete control over your communication channels.

To view the phone number settings, navigate to Settings > Phone Numbers and click on the pencil icon.

### **Number Name**

The Number Name serves as the internal identifier for the phone number. It could be something like a ‘main marketing number’ or the name of the employee assigned to the number.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761073814145-image2.png)

### Call Recording

Enabling call recording not only records calls but also publishes the recordings into the conversation stream of each contact. Be sure to enter a whisper message, such as “this call will be recorded for quality purposes,” to inform callers.

You can enable or disable the recording message as you wish. Please take into account your local regulations when deciding whether to include the call-recording message.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761075272210-image3.png)

### **Forwarding Number**

This is the phone number to which inbound calls to this new number should route. When someone calls the new number, the call will be redirected to the number you save here.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761075333724-image7.png)

This is the phone number to which inbound calls to this number should route.   

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761075470197-image9.png)

**Ring more Team Members:**

This setting allows you to designate one or more users to receive incoming calls. The calls will be forwarded to the selected users.

Note: The calls will ring on the specified channel of the user. If both the Mobile App and the Web App are available, the call will ring on the Web App.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761075753543-image10.png)

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761075796176-image11.png)

### Call Timeouts:

#### Inbound timeout:

#### This setting determines the time (in seconds) the system allows inbound calls to ring before being dropped or forwarded to a voicemail (personal voicemail or pre-recorded voicemail within the ). Adjust this based on your preferences.

-   If you want the lead to reach your cell phone voicemail, leave this field blank or add 60 seconds.
-   For a pre-recorded voicemail within , set the timeout to 1-4 seconds. Ensure you've uploaded a voicemail (MP3/WAV file) within the Business Info tab or the assigned user's profile settings.
-   If you want the line to ring and the lead to reach a pre-recorded voicemail set within , add the inbound call timeout to around 20+/- seconds. Setting a short timeout will ensure the line doesn't ring long enough to reach your personal voicemail recording.

#### Outbound timeout: 

#### This setting determines the time (in seconds) the system lets outbound calls ring before dropping the call. Set a shorter outbound timeout (around 30 seconds) for voicemail drop campaigns or when agents want to speak only to readily available leads. This is ideal if you run voicemail drop campaigns or your agents have large lists and only want to speak to readily available leads.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761075943180-image5.png)

## Advanced Settings

### Enable Call Connect

If this box is not checked, incoming calls will function as normal so that the call immediately connects when you answer. If we check this box, however, you’ll hear a whisper message prompting you to push a button on your phone to accept the incoming call. This helps prevent confusion between connected calls and voicemail recordings.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076248882-image13.png)

**Why activate this feature?**

This setting enables powerful automation, such as the Missed-Call Text-Back feature, ensuring accurate call status recognition.

To execute automation like that, the system needs to know if a call is connected, and often it can get confused by thinking that a voicemail recording is a person. When that happens, the call is categorized as connected even though it didn’t connect to a human.

We can eliminate the possibility of confusion by checking the checkbox. By checking the box, the receiver of the inbound call must press a key to connect. That way, the system always knows whether or not a call was answered by a human, which is why we recommend turning this on.

### Whisper Message

The Whisper Message is played to the person answering the call **when Call Connect features are enabled.** It informs the receiver about the call source and prompts them to press a key to connect. So we would put something like “ call” or “Call from your account name, press a key” so that you know you’re about to hop on a call from the system and that you need to press a key to connect.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076295801-image1.png)

**NOTE:** You can utilize Custom Values in your Whisper Message. You can add things like {{contact.full\_name}} to hear the name of the lead you will be connected with. 

### Pass Called Number As Caller ID

By default, the caller's number is displayed in Caller ID. Choosing this option passes the purchased number as the Caller ID, ensuring clear identification.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076356741-image12.png)

**Use Your Verified Number as Caller ID for Outbound Calls**: This feature allows you to show your verified phone number as the Caller ID when making outbound calls.|  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076438924-image14.png)

**Voice Integrity Tooltip for Unanswered Calls**

To support better outbound call performance, displays a helpful tooltip in the web dialer after an unanswered call, recommending registration with Voice Integrity.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076494940-image6.png)

### Advanced Settings:

#### Quiet Hours for Outbound Call

**Quiet Hours for Outbound Calls** prevents calls outside 8:00 AM–9:00 PM (contact’s local time) by showing a prompt in the web dialer. It helps ensure respectful outreach and better answer rates. When calling during quiet hours, a warning appears with options to cancel or proceed. Enabled by default; manage it via **_Settings → Phone Numbers → Additional Settings → Outbound Call_**

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076650541-image4.png)

**The SMS Restriction History**

The SMS Restriction History feature aims to help users monitor and track any adverse actions related to their SMS sending activities, such as daily or weekly limit violations, warning emails, and temporary restrictions based on specific criteria like opt-out rates.

**To utilize this feature**, navigate to the settings tab on the left and click on phone numbers within the settings menu bar, locate the advanced settings tab, and go to restriction history from there. Review any recorded actions. 

**Note** that users must have appropriate permissions to access and use this feature.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076706703-image15.png)

**Messaging Ramp Progress Card** 

The Messaging Ramp Progress Card helps users track and improve SMS messaging capabilities with clear insights and step-by-step guidance. It provides a detailed view of current messaging levels, future progression opportunities, and instructions to unlock higher limits, empowering users to manage their messaging limits confidently. 

Users can access this feature under **Settings > Phone Numbers > Advanced Settings > Messaging Limit**, ensuring compliance with all messaging requirements.  

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1761076750125-image8.png)

**FAQs**




**Question:** Why should I activate the Call Connect feature?
**Answer:** Activating Call Connect ensures accurate recognition of call status by requiring the receiver to press a key to connect. This prevents the system from mistaking voicemail recordings for actual human connections, which is essential for enabling automations like the Missed-Call Text-Back feature.




**Question:** What happens if I don't activate Call Connect?
**Answer:** If Call Connect is not activated, the system may mistakenly categorize calls as "connected" even if they only reached voicemail. This can disrupt call-based automation such as follow-ups or missed-call text-back sequences.


**Question:  What is a Whisper Message, and why is it important?
**Answer:** A Whisper Message is played when Call Connect is activated, informing the call receiver about the call source and prompting them to press a key to connect. It’s useful to identify the caller or the account that initiated the call, helping you prepare before answering.




**Question:** Can I customize the Whisper Message?
**Answer:** Yes, you can customize the Whisper Message using custom values such as {{contact.full\_name}} to include details about the lead, ensuring you know who you’ll be speaking with before connecting.




**Question:** How does Call Recording work, and is it legal?
**Answer:** When enabled, Call Recording logs calls and saves the recordings in the conversation stream of the contact for future reference. To comply with legal requirements, you must include a Whisper Message that informs the caller, such as "This call will be recorded for quality purposes."




**Question:** How should I set the Inbound Timeout?
**Answer:** The Inbound Timeout controls how long the system allows an incoming call to ring before routing it to voicemail. For personal voicemail, leave it blank or set it to 60 seconds. For pre-recorded voicemail within , set it to 1-4 seconds, and for a mix, set it around 20 seconds.




**Question:** What is the Outbound Timeout, and why should I set it?
**Answer:** The Outbound Timeout limits how long the system lets outbound calls ring before dropping. Setting a shorter timeout (about 30 seconds) is ideal for campaigns where you only want to reach leads who can answer immediately, like voicemail drop campaigns.




**Question:** How does the "Ring More Team Members" feature work?
**Answer:** This feature allows you to forward incoming calls to one or more users. The selected users' phones will ring on their Web App or Mobile App until one picks up, improving the chances of calls being answered.




**Question:** How can I set up the Missed-Call Text-Back automation?
**Answer:** To set up the Missed-Call Text-Back automation, activate Call Connect to ensure the system accurately tracks whether a call is connected to a human. Once this is in place, you can automate text messages for missed calls using the workflow builder.




**Question:** What is the default Inbound/Outbound timeout if no value is specified?
**Answer:** If no value is entered for the Inbound or Outbound timeout, the system defaults to 60 seconds for both.




**Question:** How can I view my SMS Restriction History?
**Answer:** You can view your SMS Restriction History by navigating to the Settings tab and selecting Phone Numbers from the settings menu. Then, go to Advanced Settings and click on Restriction History. This feature allows you to track any adverse actions related to your SMS sending activities, such as daily or weekly limit violations, warning emails, and temporary restrictions based on specific criteria like opt-out rates.**  

Question: What are "Quiet Hours for Outbound Calls," and how do they affect my dialing?**  

**Answer:** This feature prevents outbound calls from being made outside the hours of 8:00 AM to 9:00 PM in the contact's local time. If you attempt to call during these hours, a warning will appear in the web dialer with options to either cancel the call or proceed, ensuring respectful outreach.  




**Question:** What is the Messaging Ramp Progress Card, and where can I find it?

**Answer:** The Messaging Ramp Progress Card is a tool that helps you track and improve your SMS messaging limits by providing insights into your current messaging level and instructions on how to unlock higher limits. You can find it by navigating to Settings > Phone Numbers > Advanced Settings > Messaging Limit.




**Question:** What is the "Pass Called Number As Caller ID" feature, and why would I use it?
**Answer:** By default, the system displays the actual lead's phone number on your caller ID when they call you. Enabling "Pass Called Number As Caller ID" instead displays the specific business number they dialed. This is useful for instantly identifying which marketing campaign or tracking number is generating the call before you even pick up.
