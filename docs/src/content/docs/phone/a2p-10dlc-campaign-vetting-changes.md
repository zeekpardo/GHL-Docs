**A2P 10DLC Campaign Vetting Changes**

Due to an industry change that's looking to improve and reduce spam, fraud, and unwanted messaging and protect SMS as a trusted communications channel in the U.S., every messaging provider that services A2P Campaigns must conduct this vetting.

The Campaign Registry (TCR) has announced updated fees for A2P 10DLC brand registration and campaign vetting, which will take effect on August 1, 2025. This update will reflect in your billing for all new campaigns registered from this date onwards. The fee changes will apply to brand registrations and the manual vetting process but do not affect the recurring campaign fees.

**What You Need to Know Moving Forward**From August 1, 2025, all new A2P 10DLC campaign registrations will reflect the updated brand registration and vetting fees. This adjustment applies to all new campaigns.  

### **How to Navigate the Updated A2P 10DLC Campaign Process**

**1-Prepare for the New Registration Costs**Before registering new campaigns after August 1, 2025, be aware of the updated fees. This includes brand registration and manual vetting charges, which will be reflected in your billing.  

**2-Submit Your New Campaigns**The process for submitting new A2P 10DLC campaigns remains the same. Submit the necessary details for the brand registration and campaign setup, and the manual vetting process will begin.

**3-Manual Vetting Process**Once a new campaign is submitted, it will go into a "Pending" state until it is manually vetted and approved. The vetting process is designed to ensure compliance with the regulations for A2P messaging.  

**4-Monitor Campaign Status**After approval, your campaign will be eligible to send compliant messages on the A2P route. If the campaign is rejected, you’ll be informed of the necessary steps for resolution.  

### **Tips for Managing the Updated A2P 10DLC Fees**  


-   **Focus on Compliance**: Ensure your new campaigns comply with A2P 10DLC guidelines to avoid delays in the vetting process.
-   **Consider Toll-Free Messaging**: If your campaign doesn’t pass the vetting process, toll-free messaging can serve as an alternative.

### FAQs




**Question:** When will the new fees for A2P 10DLC registration be applied?
**Answer:** The updated fees will apply to all new campaign registrations from August 1, 2025, onwards.




**Question:** Are there any changes to the recurring campaign fees?
**Answer:** No, the monthly campaign fee remains the same, and it is billed separately for each active campaign.




**Question:** What happens if my campaign doesn’t pass the vetting process?
**Answer:** If your campaign fails the vetting process, the registration fee is non-refundable. Make sure your campaign complies with the necessary guidelines to avoid rejection.




**Question:** Can I use toll-free messaging if my campaign is rejected?
**Answer:** Yes, toll-free messaging remains an alternative option for A2P 10DLC messaging.




**Question:** What if my campaign is rejected after vetting?

**Answer:** You will be informed of the necessary steps to resolve issues. The registration fee is non-refundable if the campaign fails the vetting process.




**Question:** Can I appeal a rejected campaign?

**Answer:** Yes. You will receive guidance on resolving compliance issues and can resubmit the campaign once adjustments are made.  




**Question:** Do I need an EIN or Tax ID to register a Sole Proprietor Brand?

**Answer:** No. The **Sole Proprietor** option is specifically designed for small businesses or individuals who do not have an EIN, SSN, or DUNS registration. However, please note that this brand type is limited to only one phone number.




**Question:** I don’t have an EIN or Tax ID; can I still register for A2P?

**Answer:** Yes. You can select the **Sole Proprietor Brand** option. This is specifically for businesses without an EIN, SSN, or DUNS registration. However, this brand type is restricted to **one phone number only**.
