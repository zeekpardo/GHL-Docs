**A2P 10DLC best practices**

A2P 10DLC refers to a system in the United States that allows businesses to send Application-to-Person (A2P) type messaging via standard 10-digit long code (10DLC) phone numbers. A2P 10DLC offerings provide better delivery quality and lower filtering risk than long code SMS of the past, using the same phone numbers. This registration process is also available for countries outside of the U.S.

Register now to benefit from improved deliverability and increased throughput in the new verified A2P 10DLC ecosystem. 

Please follow these best practices when submitting new campaigns to help ensure your campaigns are approved for this vetting process.

**Please Note:** Don't think of these best practices as a "catch-all" that will guarantee approval if you adhere to them all; rather, think of them as a baseline that shows the overall direction that the ecosystem is heading towards when it comes to compliant, high-quality communications.

-   If the business has a Tax Number (EIN for the US, CCN for Canada, etc.), Please select "Yes, the business I’m registering has a US EIN"(Standard Low Volume Brand Registration).

-   If the business does not have a Tax Number but operates from a valid Business Address in the US/Canada, Please select "No, the business I’m registering does not have a US EIN"(Sole Prop Brand Registration).

-   If the business does not have a Tax ID and the business address is not in US/Canada, unfortunately, they cannot proceed with A2P Brand Registration. In this case, the best alternative would be to use Toll-Free numbers after completing the registration process.

-   If you have a Tax ID (EIN) and send under 6,000 message segments per day, you should register for the low-volume use case. If you have a Tax ID (EIN) and send over 6,000 message segments per day, you should register for appropriate campaign types within the Standard use case.

-   An important aspect of A2P 10DLC Brand registration is the introduction of the Standard Brand registration offering. This registration is designed for businesses that require higher daily sending limits, as approved by the Campaign Registry. To be eligible for Standard Brand registration, businesses must possess a valid Tax ID and provide other pertinent business details. The Standard Brand registration process involves associated fees, including the Brand registration fee, campaign vetting fee, and monthly campaign fees. It is crucial to understand the differences between the various Brand registration options to make an informed decision about the suitable choice for your business.

## **Understanding Trust Scores and Message Throughput**

When you complete your US A2P Brand registration, your Brand will receive a Trust Score. This rating influences the maximum message throughput that will be allocated to each of your Campaign use cases.

Trust Scores are grouped into three levels, from lowest to highest. Your Trust Score level, combined with your Campaign Type, will determine the message throughput that is allocated to your campaign use case.

Your campaign use case describes the type of messaging you plan to send. There are two important requirements under the new A2P 10DLC system: Businesses must identify who they are to the carrier networks (Brand registration). Businesses must register what type of messages they are sending, i.e. notifications (Campaign registration).

## **Forbidden use cases will result in Campaign rejection**

Make sure your A2P Campaign does not involve prohibited content such as cannabis, hate speech, etc., and that your use case is compliant with the Messaging Policy. Please view our article on messaging policy for more information.

## **Opt-In Guidelines**

To effectively collect opt-in from recipients, it is imperative to adhere to these guidelines.

We checked against rejected campaigns and found that the opt-in description lacks details in many cases.

Opt-in keywords should be **alphanumeric or accented characters**. The use of spaces between keywords, such as 'I Agree' and 'Sign UP,' is not permitted. However, one-word keywords are acceptable. Suitable options include **Subscribe, Start, Y, Yes, Confirm, and Register.**

### **As per our carrier requirements, opt-in should follow the following guidelines.**

**Website opt-in:** To collect opt-in from customers via your website, incorporate a form where clients can submit their phone numbers and consent to receive messages from your company. Communicate that submitting the phone number implies consent to message receipt. Additionally, provide a checkbox for clients to confirm their agreement.

**Website Posting (Support):** Facilitate opt-in by prominently displaying your phone number on your website. This can be done through support pages, contact sections, or call-to-action buttons. Ensure customers can easily locate the phone number to opt in and receive messages.

**Keyword or QR Code Opt-In:** Employ specific keywords or QR codes that customers can utilize to opt-in. These keywords or QR codes should be included in advertisements, social media posts, or physical materials. Instruct customers to either text the keyword or scan the QR code for opt-in.

## **Ensure data accuracy and consistency**

### **Consistency in brand, website, and sample messages**

Maintaining uniformity in brand name, website, and sample messages is essential to prevent campaign rejection. If inconsistencies arise, such as a brand name mismatch between the website and sample messages, the campaign is at risk of rejection.

For Instance: If your brand name is Store, your website is [www.store.com](http://www.store.com), but your sample messages say “Here’s your one-time passcode for logging into [www.business.com&rdquo](http://www.business.com&rdquo);, your campaign will be rejected

### **Consistency in sample messages and use cases**

Registered use cases must be accurately reflected in sample messages. Any discrepancy between registered use cases and sample messages can lead to campaign rejection.

For Instance: If you register a marketing campaign, but sample messages say “Here’s your one-time passcode: 123456”, your campaign will be rejected

### **Consistency in email domain and company name** 

Brands should use dedicated email domains to match their company name. Failure to align email domains with company names could result in campaign rejection.

For Instance: If you register a brand as Business Inc., but you provide an email address with a Gmail domain name, your campaign will be rejected.

**_Note:_** _This check only applies to large, well-known corporations that typically have dedicated email domains_

### **Make sure you submit real, working websites**

If you indicate that your customers opt-in to your messages via the website, but provide a website address that does not function, your campaign will be rejected

### **Make sure you create as few duplicative brands and campaigns as possible**

Excessive brands with the same EIN and excessive campaigns with the same campaign attributes may be seen as high-risk and may result in campaign rejection

### **If you wish to send templated messages, please make sure to indicate the templated fields in sample messages with brackets, to help reviewers better identify which parts are templated**

For example, please write “Doctor check due for \[John Doe\], Visit \[[www.business.com](http://www.business.com)\] to schedule an appointment or call \[123-456-7890\]”

## **Ensure your use case involves consumer consent before sending messages**

### **Consumer Consent and Opt-In**

Properly collecting consumer consent is a fundamental requirement for campaigns. Complying with CTIA guidelines is essential for appropriately collecting consumer consent. Detailed instructions and best practices for handling consumer consent are outlined in the CTIA guidelines document. ([https://api.ctia.org/wp-conten...](https://api.ctia.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/190719-CTIA-Messaging-Principles-and-Best-Practices-FINAL.pdf))

### **Make sure consumer opt-in is collected appropriately**

If you indicate you collect opt-in via text messages, but your sample messages say “Hi, is this the owner of 123 Maple Street? I’d like to discuss how I can help you sell your property”, it is clear that you have not collected appropriate consent before sending messages and your campaign will be rejected.

### **Make sure opt-in language is available on your website if you indicated in the “message flow” field that a consumer opts into your campaign on your company website**

If your brand "Store" uses its website, [www.store.com](http://www.store.com), to collect phone numbers but your website does not contain opt-in language such as “By providing your phone number, you agree to receive text messages from STORE. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies.”, your campaign will be rejected.

**Opt-In Language Availability:** For campaigns that indicate consumer opt-in via the company website, it is crucial to include opt-in language on the website. This language should communicate the consent process clearly, such as "By providing your phone number, you agree to receive text messages from \[Company Name\]. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies." Ensuring alignment between brand practices and website content is essential to avoid campaign rejection.

### **We recommend having opt-out language in at least one of your sample messages**

For example, please consider adding language such as “Please reply STOP to opt-out” in one of your sample messages

## **Toll-Free Messaging as an Alternative**

While A2P 10DLC offers enhanced deliverability and lower filtering risk, Toll-Free messaging remains a viable alternative. Toll-free messaging also requires a verification process but does not rely on Brand and Campaign Registration. Depending on your business needs and messaging volume, Toll-Free messaging could be an attractive option to consider.

## Messaging Registration and Verification Overview

This standardizes messaging requirements across domestic and international routes within to make message delivery more predictable, compliant, and easier to manage over time. By reducing unnecessary setup steps while maintaining required safeguards, it helps ensure messages are sent successfully with fewer interruptions.

## Simplified Messaging Requirements by Region

Messaging requirements have been streamlined across domestic and international routes to reduce setup complexity while maintaining compliance.

-   **Canada-to-Canada Messaging:**Numbers purchased before a defined cutoff date may continue sending without A2P registration. Numbers purchased after that date can be sent using either brand registration or identity verification.
-   **U.S./Canada to International Messaging:**Messages sent internationally only require identity verification. A2P registration is not mandatory for these routes.
-   **Domestic Messaging (U.S., Canada, Puerto Rico):**Messaging between these regions still requires A2P registration. Missing registration will result in message failure.
-   **U.S./Canada to United Kingdom:**Messaging to the UK from the U.S./Canada is not supported and will fail. Domestic UK-to-UK messaging remains supported.

## FAQs




**Question:** What is A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** A2P 10DLC (Application-to-Person 10-Digit Long Code) is a system in the United States that allows businesses to send A2P-type messaging via standard 10-digit long code phone numbers. It provides better delivery quality and lower filtering risk than traditional long-code SMS.




**Question:** Is A2P 10DLC registration available outside the United States?
**Answer:** Yes, the registration process for A2P 10DLC is also available for countries outside of the U.S.




**Question:** What are the benefits of registering for A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** Registering for A2P 10DLC improves deliverability, reduces filtering risk, and increases message throughput in the verified A2P 10DLC ecosystem.




**Question:** What should I consider before registering my campaign under A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** Ensure your campaigns comply with the Messaging Policy, do not involve prohibited content, maintain consistency in branding, sample messages, and use cases, and properly collect opt-in from recipients.




**Question:** What are the types of A2P 10DLC Brand registration?
**Answer:** There are two main types of A2P 10DLC Brand registration:

-   **Low Volume Brand Registration:** For businesses with a valid Tax ID (EIN) sending under 6,000 message segments per day.
-   **Standard Brand Registration:** For businesses with a valid Tax ID sending over 6,000 message segments per day, requiring higher daily sending limits.




**Question:** What is a Trust Score, and how does it affect my messaging?
**Answer:** A Trust Score is a rating received after US A2P Brand registration that influences the maximum message throughput allocated to each of your campaign use cases. Trust Scores are grouped into three levels, from lowest to highest.




**Question:** What are the requirements for A2P 10DLC Brand registration?
**Answer:** Requirements include business name, physical address, business identity, type, status, business registration number, industry, website, regions of operations, and authorized representative details.




**Question:** Can a business without a US EIN register for A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** Yes, if the business operates from a valid address in the US or Canada, it can register under the Sole Prop Brand Registration. Businesses without a Tax ID and address outside of US/Canada cannot proceed with A2P Brand Registration and should consider Toll-Free numbers instead.




**Question:** What are the opt-in guidelines for A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** Opt-in guidelines include using website forms, displaying phone numbers on support pages, or employing keywords/QR codes for opt-in. Consent must be clearly communicated, and sample messages should include opt-out instructions like "Reply STOP to opt-out."




**Question:** What happens if my A2P campaign involves prohibited content?
**Answer:** Campaigns involving prohibited content such as cannabis or hate speech will be rejected.




**Question:** How do I ensure my campaign gets approved?
**Answer:** Adhere to best practices such as maintaining consistency in brand, website, sample messages, and use cases, providing accurate opt-in language on websites, and using real, functioning websites for opt-in methods.




**Question:** What is the difference between Standard Brand and Low Volume Brand registration?
**Answer:Low Volume Brand Registration** is for businesses with a Tax ID sending under 6,000 message segments per day. **Standard Brand Registration** is for those sending over 6,000 message segments per day and includes higher sending limits and associated fees.




**Question:** What are the costs involved in Standard Brand registration?
**Answer:** Standard Brand registration involves associated fees, including the Brand registration fee, campaign vetting fee, and monthly campaign fees.




**Question:** Can I use Toll-Free messaging instead of A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** Yes, Toll-Free messaging is an alternative that offers similar benefits without requiring Brand and Campaign Registration, although it still requires a verification process.




**Question:** Why is it important to maintain consistency in sample messages and use cases?
**Answer:** Inconsistencies between registered use cases and sample messages can lead to campaign rejection. Ensure that sample messages accurately reflect the registered use case to avoid this issue.

**Question:** What are the prohibited content types in A2P 10DLC?
**Answer:** Content related to illegal activities, hate speech, adult material, or other restricted content will lead to campaign rejection. Always review the messaging policy for specific guidelines.




**Question:** Can I use A2P 10DLC for cold outreach?
**Answer:** No. Cold outreach without explicit opt-in is **prohibited** and will result in campaign rejection or suspension. All A2P messaging must be permission-based.




**Question:** Can I send messages from the U.S. or Canada to the United Kingdom?
**Answer:** No. Messaging from the U.S./Canada to the UK is **not supported** and these messages will fail. However, domestic UK-to-UK messaging remains fully supported within the platform.




**Question:** Are there specific rules for the keywords customers use to opt-in?

**Answer:** Yes. Opt-in keywords must be **one-word and alphanumeric** (or accented). Spaces are not permitted; for example, "Subscribe" or "Confirm" are acceptable, but "Sign UP" or "I Agree" are not.
