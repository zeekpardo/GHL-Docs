**How to intregrate Adyen payments**

This article provides a clear overview of how Adyen can be used as a payment provider within . The goal is to help teams understand the capabilities, setup steps, and advantages of enabling Adyen for card payments especially for businesses operating in Europe and North America. With enterprise-grade reliability and broad regional coverage, Adyen offers a flexible solution for organizations seeking secure and scalable payment processing.

## Key Features and Benefits

### 1\. Card Payments Across EU & North America

Adyen supports credit and debit card processing, making it suitable for companies serving customers in regions where reliable payment infrastructure is essential.

### 2\. Enterprise-Level Performance

Adyen is widely recognized for robust security, strong compliance standards, and performance suitable for high-volume businesses.

### 3\. Compatibility With Existing Users

Many organizations already work with Adyen. By supporting it directly, allows these businesses to connect their existing accounts without additional onboarding processes.

### 4\. Security With 3DS Support

Adyen’s ability to process 3D Secure-enabled cards adds an extra layer of protection for international and higher-risk transactions.

### 5\. Improved Client Acquisition

Supporting a widely used payment provider helps businesses expand into enterprise markets and onboard clients more quickly.

## How to Connect Adyen for Card Payments

### Step 1: Prepare Your Adyen Merchant Account

Ensure you have:

-   An active merchant account
-   Approval from Adyen
-   Required credentials (live or test)

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763636999270-unnamed - 2025-11-20T190948.763.png>)

### Step 2: Access Payment Settings

In your account, navigate to:  
**Dashboard → Payments → Connect Adyen**

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763637020390-unnamed - 2025-11-20T191010.368.png>)

### Step 3: Enter Credentials

Add the necessary credential details:

**Live Mode**

-   Company API Key
-   Live URL Prefix

**Test/Sandbox Mode**

-   Company API Key

After entering the correct information, select **Save**.

![](<https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1763637037668-unnamed - 2025-11-20T191029.907.png>)

### Step 4: Set as Default Payment Provider

Assign Adyen as the default provider in the channels where card payments should be accepted.

### Step 5: Begin Accepting Payments

Once connected, Adyen-enabled payment links, checkout flows, and card processing will become available for use within .

## FAQs




**Question:** What do I need before connecting Adyen?
**Answer:** You need an active Adyen merchant account, required credentials, and approval from Adyen to enable payments.




**Question:** Which payment methods are supported right now?
**Answer:** Currently, only card payments (credit and debit) are supported. Additional methods may be evaluated for future phases.




**Question:** Where do I enter my credentials?
**Answer:** Go to Dashboard → Payments → Connect Adyen and enter your live or test credentials.




**Question:** Can I test the integration before going live?
**Answer:** Yes. You can connect your sandbox key to test payment flows before activating live mode.




**Question:** Who determines pricing and availability?
**Answer:** All pricing, underwriting decisions, and regional availability are managed directly by Adyen.  




**Question:** Does this integration support 3D Secure (3DS) transactions?
**Answer:** Yes. Adyen’s integration supports 3D Secure-enabled cards, providing an additional layer of security for international and higher-risk transactions.




**Question:** Where can I see a list of successful or failed Adyen transactions?

**Answer:** You can monitor all transaction activity directly in your **Payments > Transactions** tab. For deeper financial reporting, dispute management, or detailed payout schedules, you should refer to your **Adyen Customer Area** dashboard.




**Question:** Can I use my existing Adyen account, or do I need to create a new one for this platform?

**Answer:** You can use your existing account. The integration is designed to allow organizations already working with Adyen to connect their current accounts directly without going through a new onboarding process.
