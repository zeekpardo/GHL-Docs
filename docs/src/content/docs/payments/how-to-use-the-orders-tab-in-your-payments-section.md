The Orders tab allows you to manage all of your Orders. You can view a sortable, searchable list of all Orders, which you may also download via an Excel file.

This list gives you a history of all orders that may be important for your accounting purposes, managing metrics and goals for your team, planning business initiatives, and more.

You can customize the information displayed within the list of all Orders in the payments tab.

**Abandoned Checkouts on the Orders Page**

Business users can track abandoned cart orders directly on their online stores' Orders page. This helps businesses recover lost sales opportunities and better understand customer behavior.

Accessing Abandoned Checkouts:

1\. Navigate to Orders:

-   Go to the Payments section on the left.
-   Select Orders at the top.
-   From the dropdown click on Abandoned Checkouts.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXeUI2LjRH6pEojN3VyeqzNq-8jZgb4XHoDODemCC9FoJpdQ6G1WaTQZW-uFOqi74StpZH7kw88VXVKxTR8Em7hv8N3BtHElwhuLP-jqQO0OK4do5UmqIe3DCVfapk3qJYR0lFFP6Q?key=sl4boahjoNuH2_e1CX44pg)

2\. Viewing Customer Details:

-   The Abandoned Checkouts section will display customer details, including the items they added to their cart.

3\. Email Status:

-   Each order will include an Email status indicating whether the customer has received an automatic notification about the abandoned cart.
-   You can configure these automatic notifications by going to Settings and then Notifications.

4\. Recovered Orders:

-   If an abandoned order is recovered, it will be marked as recovered, providing clear visibility of successful recoveries.

### **Partial Payment Support via Orders API**

-   Businesses can also collect partial payments through checkouts, allowing customers to pay in installments for one-time products.
-   The "Collect Payment" CTA will appear on the Order Details page, enabling payments via manual methods, card on file, or a new card. Initially available for calendar bookings, with support for one-time products only, not for subscription products.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfc523LZfoJTCrA7m1uteSzmn-7r8qxu-65flOq3-ZWESMlU6dW_bnycPy2mm2kSEpXtwv-JPxpPHMo2lt15wpzSXgXuqmlQp_nQsH-Cv9Y8NXqoBb6QrV2DjRaMNH35P6p_wZmqg?key=sl4boahjoNuH2_e1CX44pg)

-   An example of this would be when a customer orders a custom-made suit worth $500. They can pay a $100 deposit to confirm the order, and the remaining $400 can be collected either manually or using the customer's card on file/new card when they pick up the suit from the store.

## FAQs




**Question:** What is the purpose of the Orders tab?
**Answer:** The Orders tab allows you to manage your orders by viewing, sorting, and searching through a list of all orders. It also provides the option to download this list as an Excel file, which can be used for accounting, managing metrics, planning business initiatives, and more.




**Question:** What order statuses can I view in the Orders tab?
**Answer:** You can view orders by status: all, pending, completed, or canceled. This helps you easily track the current state of all orders.




**Question:** Can I filter orders by payment mode?
**Answer:** You can filter orders by payment mode, including all, live mode, or test mode. This feature helps you differentiate between actual and test transactions made during setup or troubleshooting.




**Question:** What sources of orders can I filter in the Orders tab?
**Answer:** You can filter orders by source: funnel, website, or membership. This allows you to see which channels are generating the most orders.




**Question:** How can I search for orders within a specific date range?
**Answer:** You can search for orders by selecting a specific date range, such as January 1, 2022, to January 31, 2022. This helps you analyze orders within a particular timeframe.




**Question:** What information is included when I download the orders list as a CSV file?
**Answer:** The CSV file will include detailed information such as the item name, quantity, price, discount, subtotal, product ID, and price ID. This provides a comprehensive view of each order for analysis or record-keeping.




**Question:** What is the Abandoned Checkouts feature on the Orders page?
**Answer:** The Abandoned Checkouts feature allows business users to track orders that were started but not completed, helping to recover lost sales opportunities and understand customer behavior.




**Question:** What new details are added to the CSV exports?
**Answer:** Fields like payment method, currency, taxes, discounts, refund status, and more are added for better data insights.




**Question:** Why was the field name changed from "Amount" to "Total"?
**Answer:** To maintain an industry-standard naming convention across all exports.




**Question:** How do I access the updated CSV exports?
**Answer:** Go to Payments, then Transactions, Subscriptions, or Orders, and use the download icon to export the data.




**Question:** How can I track abandoned checkouts?
**Answer:** You can track abandoned checkouts in the "Abandoned Checkouts" section under Orders. It shows customer details, email status, and marks recovered orders for easy tracking.




**Question:** What information is needed to collect a manual or partial payment?
**Answer:** A contact order record must exist first. Payments can then be collected using saved payment methods or new ones through the Order Details view.




**Question:** Does the system support partial payments for subscription-based products?

**Answer:** No. Partial payment support is currently available only for one-time products (such as calendar bookings) and is not supported for subscription products.  




**Question:** If I delete a funnel or a product, will the order history be deleted too?

**Answer:** No. Order records are permanent financial entries. Even if the source funnel or the product itself is deleted from your system, the historical data in the Orders tab remains intact for your accounting and tax records.




**Question:** How do I know if an automated email was actually sent to a lead who abandoned their cart?

**Answer:** In the Abandoned Checkouts section, each entry includes an **Email Status**. This indicator tells you whether the system has already triggered the automatic notification to that specific customer.
