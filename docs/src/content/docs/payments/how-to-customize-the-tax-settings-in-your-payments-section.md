Managing taxes on invoices, order forms, and products is now more structured and efficient with enhanced tax display and automated tax calculation features. These tools provide **a clear tax breakdown, customizable product-level tax settings, and automatic tax calculations based on customer location** for US-based sellers. Businesses can apply taxes to products, configure tax settings, and view tax details in invoices and order summaries with greater accuracy and ease.

**International Automatic Taxes (Including VAT)**

Businesses can configure and apply automatic taxes for multiple countries, ensuring accurate tax calculations across global checkouts, including country-specific **VAT IDs or local tax ID numbers**.

**Supported Checkout Types:** Funnels, payment links, invoices, and ecommerce stores, and automatically applies taxes based on the customer's address – for all payment providers.

Configure your **nexus addresses** by adding countries or states where you are required to collect taxes, and ensure your Tax ID is entered for country-specific calculations. A list of countries where automatic tax calculations would work is available in the list of Nexus Addresses.

 For the US and Canada, respective states should be added wherever tax calculation is required. A business can also **enter its Tax ID when adding a nexus address for a specific country**. If there are additional tax benefits or applications based on the ID, it would be considered at the checkout while calculating the taxes.

**FAQs**




**Question:** How is tax displayed on invoices and order forms?
**Answer:** Taxes are shown in a dedicated column displaying the cumulative total, with a detailed breakdown available in the Order Summary.  




**Question:** Can I set different tax rates for individual products?
**Answer:** Yes, you can specify tax rates per product under **Payments > Products**, or set a default tax category in **Payments > Settings > Taxes**.  




**Question:** What happens if I delete a tax rate?
**Answer:** Deleting a tax rate will automatically remove it from all associated products. You may need to update product tax settings manually if required.  




**Question:** Can I adjust tax settings after an invoice is created?
**Answer:** Yes, but changes will only apply to future invoices. Previously issued invoices will retain the tax settings that were in place at the time of creation.  




**Question:** How do I configure automatic taxes for multiple countries?
**Answer:** In the tax settings, you can select multiple countries and add the respective nexus addresses where taxes need to be calculated. For each country, you can also add the relevant VAT or local tax ID.  




**Question:** What are nexus addresses, and why are they important?
**Answer:** Nexus addresses determine where your business has a tax obligation. You need to configure these addresses for each country or state where you are required to collect taxes.  


**Question:  What happens if I need to delete a country or state from my nexus addresses?
**Answer:** Deleting a country or state must be done individually, but removing a country like Canada with added states will also delete the nexus address status for those states.  




**Question:** Can I disable taxes for specific products or invoices?
**Answer:** Yes. You can turn off taxes at the **product level** by deselecting “Charge Tax on This Product.” For individual invoices or order forms, you can manually remove or adjust tax lines if needed.  


**Question:  Can I configure product-level tax settings globally for all products?
**Answer:** Yes, you can configure global tax settings under Payments > Settings > Taxes, where  you can set products as tax-inclusive or tax-exclusive. If no selection is made at the global level, the default is tax-exclusive. Existing products will default to "As per Global settings" but can be individually adjusted.




**Question:** Does the system automatically know the tax rate for a customer in another state?
**Answer:** Yes, if you have enabled **Automatic Taxes** and added that state as a **Nexus Address**. The system uses the customer's address entered on the order form or invoice to calculate the exact local tax rate in real-time.




**Question:** How can I see a detailed breakdown of how tax totals were calculated on a specific invoice?

**Answer:** In the invoice editor, you can find a breakdown in the Order Summary section. Additionally, you can hover your cursor over the tax values to see tooltips that provide specific details on every tax applied to that particular item.
