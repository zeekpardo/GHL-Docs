**Question:Are there any specific requirements for integrating NMI?
**Answer:** Specific integration requirements will vary based on your system and the merchant service provider you choose. Generally, you will need to follow NMI’s integration documentation and work closely with your provider for a smooth setup.




**Question:** Can I use NMI in countries not listed in the supported countries section?
**Answer:** NMI currently supports a wide range of countries, but if your country is not listed, you may not be able to use NMI's services for payment processing directly. However, NMI may have plans to expand into more countries, so it’s recommended to contact NMI directly for the most up-to-date information regarding availability in your region.




**Question:** Can I switch acquirers after setting up NMI?
**Answer:** Switching acquirers is possible but may require re-integration depending on your system and the new provider’s requirements. Consult NMI documentation or your provider before making changes.




**Question:** What are the main steps to begin using NMI for my business payments?

**Answer:** To start using NMI, you should first contact NMI via their website to set up an account. Next, identify a compatible merchant service provider in your country from NMI's supported list. Then, follow NMI’s specific integration steps to link the gateway to your system. Finally, once the configuration is complete, you can begin processing payments.




**Question:** What is "Load Balancing" within NMI, and how does it benefit my business?

**Answer:** Load Balancing is an intelligent routing feature that allows you to distribute transaction volume across multiple merchant accounts (MIDs). This is useful for managing risk, staying under monthly processing limits for a specific provider, or routing transactions to different acquirers based on the product type or sales volume.




**Question:** What is the first step I should take if I want to find a provider in my specific country?

**Answer:** You should first review the **"Supported Countries"** list in the guide to identify which acquirers operate in your region, then reach out to NMI via their website to begin the account setup process.
