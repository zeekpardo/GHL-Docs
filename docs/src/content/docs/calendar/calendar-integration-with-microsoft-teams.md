**Calendar Integration with Microsoft Teams**

Integrating your Microsoft Teams account with your calendar provides an efficient way to manage meetings, streamline scheduling, and eliminate manual steps in generating meeting links. By setting Microsoft Teams as a meeting location in your calendar settings, you can automatically generate dynamic Teams links for each new booking, simplifying the process for both you and your attendees. This article will guide you through the integration process, highlight the key features, and offer practical tips for using this feature effectively.

**Key Features and Benefits**

-   **Automatic Meeting Links**: Once integrated, Microsoft Teams will automatically generate a unique meeting link for every new booking, removing the need for manual link creation.
-   **Seamless Integration**: Whether you are scheduling personal meetings or managing round-robin sessions, you can easily add Microsoft Teams as the default meeting location.
-   **Increased Productivity**: By automating the process of link generation, you reduce administrative time, allowing you to focus on what truly matters—your meetings.
-   **Customizable for Each User**: Each user can connect their own Microsoft Teams account, ensuring that meetings are tailored to their schedules and preferences.

**How to Use Microsoft Teams Integration**

Follow these simple steps to connect Microsoft Teams to your calendar and start generating dynamic meeting links:

**1\. Connecting Your Microsoft Teams Account**

-   Navigate to **Settings > My Profile>Calendar settings>Connect Microsoft teams**
-   Under the **Video Conferencing** tab, click **Add New**.
-   Select **Microsoft Teams** and click **Connect**.

**2\. How to Add Microsoft Teams Links to Your Calendar**

-   After connecting your Teams account, navigate to **Calendar Settings** and edit the calendar you want to modify.
-   Go to the **Meeting Details** tab and scroll to the **Team Members** section.
-   Choose the team member and ensure they have successfully integrated Microsoft Teams.
-   Select **Microsoft Teams** as the meeting location and click **Save**.
-   Now, every new booking will automatically generate a unique Teams link and send it along with the calendar invite.
-   You can also connect through **Calendars > Calendar Settings > Connections**, then follow the same steps to connect Microsoft Teams.

**3\. Managing Multiple Accounts**

-   If you manage several calendars or work with multiple team members, you can easily assign Microsoft Teams as the default location for various calendar setups, such as **Personal Booking** or **Round Robin**.
-   Each team member can connect their own Microsoft Teams account, allowing personalized meeting setups.
-   You can assign Microsoft Teams as the default location for specific calendar types like **Round Robin** and **Service Calendars**, enabling automatic link generation for each meeting.

**Important Notes**

-   Connecting a Microsoft Outlook calendar does **not** automatically link Microsoft Teams. Ensure you separately connect Microsoft Teams through the **Video Conferencing** settings.
-   Only Microsoft Teams accounts for **Work and School** (Organization and Education) are eligible for integration.
-   You can connect only **one Microsoft Teams account** per account, but the same account can be used across multiple accounts if assigned to the same user.

**Pro Tips**

-   **Optimize Scheduling**: Use this feature with round-robin calendars to distribute meeting slots among team members efficiently, ensuring that each team member’s Microsoft Teams integration is properly set up.
-   **Customizing Invitations**: When you connect Microsoft Teams, your calendar invitations can automatically include meeting links, providing a seamless booking experience for your clients or colleagues.
-   **Static Links for Event Calendars**: If you're using **Event Calendars**, you can manually generate a static Microsoft Teams link for all future bookings. Simply generate the link in Teams, and use it as a **Custom Meeting Location** in your event calendar.

**FAQs**




**Question:** Which calendars support Microsoft Teams integration?
**Answer:** Personal Booking, Round Robin, Collective, and Service Calendars support dynamic Teams links for each new booking. For Event Calendars, you can manually add a static Teams link.




**Question:** Do I need to reconnect Microsoft Teams if I integrate Outlook?
**Answer:** Yes, connecting an Outlook calendar does not automatically link Microsoft Teams. You need to connect Microsoft Teams separately under **Video Conferencing** settings.




**Question:** Can I connect multiple Microsoft Teams accounts?
**Answer:** Each user can connect only one Microsoft Teams account per account. However, the same integration can be used across multiple accounts for the same user.




**Question:** What happens if I change my Microsoft Teams credentials?
**Answer:** If you update your Teams login credentials, you may need to re-authenticate the integration in the calendar settings to ensure seamless link generation.




**Question:** Can I customize the meeting links for different calendars?
**Answer:** Yes, you can assign Microsoft Teams as the default meeting location for various calendars, ensuring each calendar generates unique links tailored to your scheduling needs.




**Question:** Can I customize calendar invites to include Team links?
**Answer:** Yes. After connecting Teams, meeting invites automatically include Teams links. You can further customize email templates for a seamless booking experience.




**Question:** Do I need a Microsoft Teams account for integration?
**Answer:** Yes, only Microsoft Teams accounts for Work and School (Organization and Education) are eligible for integration.




**Question:** Does this integration create an event on my Teams calendar too?

**Answer:** Yes. When a booking is made, it will appear on your integrated Outlook/Teams calendar, and the "Join" button will be available directly within your Microsoft interface.




**Question:** How do I ensure a specific team member's Teams link is used in a calendar?

**Answer:** When editing a calendar, go to the **Meeting Details** tab and scroll to **Team Members**. Select the specific user and then choose **Microsoft Teams** as the meeting location for that individual.
