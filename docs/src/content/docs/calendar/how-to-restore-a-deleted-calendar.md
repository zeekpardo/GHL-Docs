The "Restore Calendar" feature allows users to recover deleted calendars and their associated appointments with ease. This functionality addresses a common issue where calendars and appointments are unintentionally deleted, offering a simple way to undo such actions.

**Important Points to Note**

-   Deleting a calendar will also delete all appointments associated with it.
-   Appointments deleted within ±30 minutes of the calendar deletion will be restored.
-   Appointments deleted outside of this 30-minute window cannot be recovered.
-   Audit logs are kept for a maximum of 60 days, so it’s important to restore deleted calendars within this timeframe to prevent data loss.

For example, if a calendar is deleted on May 15th at 10:00 AM, all appointments for that calendar will also be deleted immediately. These appointments can be restored within 30 minutes from the deletion time. However, any appointments deleted before May 15th at 9:00 AM will not be restored. This process ensures only appointments deleted due to the calendar deletion are restored, not those deleted at other times.

## **How-to Steps**

### **Using the Restore Calendar Feature**

1.  **Navigate to Audit Logs:**
    -   Go to Settings > Audit Logs.
2.  **Identify the Deleted Calendar:**
    -   Locate the log entry corresponding to the deleted calendar.

## FAQs




**Question:** Why can't I restore a calendar that was deleted over 60 days ago?

**Answer:** Audit logs are only retained for 60 days. Calendars deleted beyond this period cannot be restored. Ensure timely restoration to avoid data loss.




**Question:** Can I restore appointments deleted outside the 30-minute timeframe?

**Answer:** No, only appointments deleted within ±30 minutes of the calendar deletion time will be restored. It’s important to be aware of this timeframe when restoring calendars.




**Question:** What happens if I restore a calendar with conflicting appointments?

**Answer:** Restored appointments will be added back to the calendar. If there are any conflicts, review and resolve them manually.




**Question:** Why is there a 30-minute limit for appointment restoration?
**Answer:** This ensures that only appointments deleted due to the calendar deletion are restored, avoiding unintended recovery of unrelated deleted appointments.




**Question:** Are notifications sent when a calendar is restored?
**Answer:** This depends on your platform’s notification settings. Typically, any alerts or notifications tied to restored appointments will resume based on their original settings.




**Question:** Can I choose to restore only the calendar and not the appointments?
**Answer:** No. The restoration process is designed to maintain data integrity by recovering the calendar and its associated appointments simultaneously. If you only need the calendar, you can manually delete the restored appointments afterward.




**Question:** If I accidentally restore the wrong calendar, can I "Undo" the restoration?

**Answer:** There is no "Undo" for a restoration, but you can simply delete the calendar again. This will create a new "Delete" entry in your Audit Logs, starting a fresh 60-day window and a fresh 30-minute appointment recovery window.




**Question:** Where exactly do I go to find the "Audit Logs"?
**Answer:** You can find the logs by clicking the **Settings** (gear icon) at the bottom left of your sidebar, then selecting **Audit Logs** from the menu. To find your calendar faster, use the "Filter" or "Search" bar at the top and type the name of the calendar you are looking for.
