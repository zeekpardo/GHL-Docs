Managing rooms and equipment is essential to running a service-based business efficiently. Whether you operate a salon, clinic, or fitness center, ensuring that rooms and equipment are available and adequately scheduled is critical to delivering high-quality service.

This article provides an in-depth look into how to manage rooms and equipment using advanced scheduling features. With these tools, you can optimize resource allocation, prevent double bookings, and ensure a smooth service experience for your clients.

**Key Features and Benefits**

-   **Room Creation and Management**: Assign rooms to your appointments to avoid overlapping bookings and ensure each service has a designated space.
-   **Equipment Allocation**: Keep track of equipment availability and prevent overbooking by linking equipment to service calendars.
-   **Seamless Integration with Calendars**: Automatically book rooms and equipment when scheduling services, eliminating manual management.
-   **Double-Booking Prevention**: The system cross-checks the availability of users and rooms/equipment, ensuring no resource conflicts.

**How to Use Rooms and Equipment**

1.  **Enable Rooms and Equipment Feature**:  
    Navigate to **Calendars > Settings** in .  
    Under **Preferences**, toggle on the **Rooms and Equipment** option. This will enable the ability to create and manage both rooms and equipment for scheduling.

1.  **Creating a Room**:
    -   Go to the new **Rooms** tab that appears after enabling the feature.
    -   Click on **Create Room**.
    -   Provide details like:
        -   **Room Name**: Assign a unique name (e.g., "Massage Room").
        -   **Description**: Briefly describe the room's purpose.
        -   **Room Capacity**: Specify the maximum number of appointments the room can handle simultaneously.
    -   Associate the room with one or more service calendars. The room will automatically be reserved when an appointment is booked on these calendars.

1.  **Creating Equipment**:
    -   In the **Calendar Settings**, click on the new **Equipments** tab.
    -   Click on **Create Equipment**.
    -   Enter details such as:
        -   **Equipment Name**: Assign a unique name (e.g., "Laser Machine").
        -   **Description**: Explain the equipment's purpose.
        -   **Total Quantity**: Indicate how many units are available.
        -   **Out-of-Service Quantity**: Track equipment that is not currently operational.
    -   Link the equipment to specific calendars, ensuring it's available for the corresponding services.

1.  **Avoiding Double Bookings**:
    -   The system will check both user and resource availability before finalizing any booking. For example, if a room has a capacity of 2 and is already booked for two services, no more appointments can be scheduled for that room until it becomes available.
    -   Similarly, if a piece of equipment is already booked, it won’t be available for other appointments until it’s free again.

**Special Considerations**:

-   **Room and Equipment Details**: You can view which room and equipment are linked to an appointment by checking the details in the appointment modal. This makes it easier to manage resources.
-   **Room Selection for Service Calendars**: During the appointment booking process, users can select a specific room, providing greater control over the allocation of rooms and equipment.

**Pro Tips**:

-   **Ensure Accurate Capacity Settings**: When setting up rooms and equipment, be precise with room capacity and available units. This helps avoid overbooking and ensures the best use of resources.
-   **Regularly Update Equipment Status**: To avoid scheduling conflicts, keep the "Out of Service" quantity current. Marking broken or unavailable equipment helps streamline operations and prevents service delays.

**FAQs**




**Question:** Can I link multiple pieces of equipment to a single calendar?

**Answer:** No, each calendar can only be associated with one piece of equipment. However, a single piece of equipment can be linked to multiple calendars.




**Question:** How does the system prevent double bookings?

**Answer:** The system cross-checks both user availability and the availability of rooms or equipment. If a resource is already booked, the time slot will be marked as unavailable for further bookings.




**Question:** What happens if an equipment is marked as "Out of Service"?

**Answer:** Equipment marked as "Out of Service" will not be available for bookings. This helps avoid scheduling appointments that require equipment that is unavailable or in need of repair.




**Question:** Can I set different room capacities for different service calendars?
**Answer:** No, the room capacity applies to all associated calendars. For instance, if a room has a capacity of 2, it can accommodate up to two appointments simultaneously across any calendars linked to it.




**Question:** Is there a way to track which room or equipment is assigned to an appointment?
**Answer:** You can view the room and equipment details in the appointment modal under the details view, which helps track the resources assigned to each appointment.  
**Question:Will rooms and equipment automatically release when an appointment is canceled?
**Answer:** Yes. When an appointment is canceled or rescheduled, the associated room and equipment are automatically freed up for new bookings.  


**Question:What is the benefit of linking rooms and equipment to service calendars?
**Answer:** Linking ensures automatic reservation of resources when appointments are booked, streamlining scheduling, preventing double bookings, and optimizing resource utilization.




**Question:** Can I assign a room to an appointment manually after it has been booked?
**Answer:** Yes. If a room wasn't automatically assigned or if you need to move a client to a different space, you can open the appointment modal in the calendar and select a different available room from the dropdown menu.




**Question:** Can I describe what a room is for so my team knows which one to book?

**Answer:** Yes. When creating a room, you can provide a **Description** to briefly explain the room's purpose (e.g., "Consultation Room" vs. "Massage Room") to ensure it is used correctly.
