Creating a dashboard using pre-existing templates is a quick and efficient way to maintain consistency and save time. This guide provides step-by-step instructions to help you navigate the process seamlessly.

## **Why Use Templates for Dashboards?**

Templates simplify the creation of dashboards by providing a pre-built structure. They help ensure uniformity across accounts, save time, and make it easy to replicate designs for different clients or purposes.

## **Step-by-Step Guide to Creating a New Dashboard Using Templates**

### **Step 1: Navigate to the Dashboard Page**

Go to the dashboard section of your account. This is where you can manage all your dashboards.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1774463524876-image4.png)

### **Step 2: Click on Add Dashboard**

Locate the blue dashboard icon and click on it. From the dropdown menu, select **Add Dashboard** to begin the creation process.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1774463543562-image3.png)

### **Step 3: Select From Template Library**

In the options presented, choose **From Template Library**. This will allow you to access a library of pre-designed dashboard templates.

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfM-dljL-Q3WHx63HoQOGV-SoEJ8M7ID37jM6zNrWoHsSLpXYY2WPOkPOgjx4BLNv1970ivqrfzbNeG4n0nc5FNhaHhZbagOF5tC3dtBJB49JfFoctYHgiA9zYvUgfUS3PquRUePg?key=zQ5fx7ohjlgz3Q6HOeLAt5O_)

### **Step 4: Choose the Template**

Browse through the available templates in the library. Select the one that best suits your needs and click **Continue**.

![](https://assets2.modalsupport.net/1774463588095-image1.png)

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXfi1MZCZU3SmeYAu1PNAiGiPcDH5j1Jd7SPazpkX0x2FtzCTKUlrXs_uxK9f2U9maRnbPHMR_e50wP8jNsPslpA5JEfjOmSqAGNCG-nQGswdKbvusTXMkEK7utIeTTX5xACFWB6?key=zQ5fx7ohjlgz3Q6HOeLAt5O_)

### **Step 5: Save the Dashboard**

Review and customize the dashboard as needed. Once satisfied, click **Save** to finalize your new dashboard.

### **Pro Tips**

-   **Customize Before Saving:** While templates provide a strong starting point, adjust the layout, widgets, or design elements to suit your specific requirements.
-   **Tag and Organize Dashboards:** Use clear naming conventions or tags to make dashboards easier to find later.
-   **Preview the Template:** Before making extensive changes, preview the template to ensure it aligns with your needs.

### **Use Cases**

1.  **Client Onboarding:** Create uniform dashboards for new clients using pre-existing templates.
2.  **Project Tracking:** Quickly set up project monitoring dashboards with standardized metrics.
3.  **Performance Reporting:** Use templates to generate dashboards for team performance and key analytics.

### **FAQs**




**Question:** Can I modify a template after creating the dashboard?

**Answer:** Yes, you can customize the dashboard after applying the template to suit your requirements.




**Question:** Are templates reusable across multiple accounts?

**Answer:** Yes, templates are designed to be reusable and can be applied to various accounts or projects.




**Question:** What if I don’t find a template that fits my needs?

**Answer:** You can create a new template by customizing an existing dashboard and saving it to the Template Library.




**Question:** Is there a limit to how many templates I can use?

**Answer:** No, you can use as many templates as needed to create your dashboards.




**Question:** Can I create a dashboard without using a template?

**Answer:** Yes, dashboards can be created from scratch if a template is not required.




**Question:** Should I customize templates before saving?

**Answer:** Yes, preview and adjust the template as needed before saving to ensure it aligns perfectly with your goals and metrics.




**Question:** Do templates include pre-configured data sources?

**Answer:** Templates may include predefined widgets and metrics, but data sources can usually be adjusted or reconnected based on your account setup  




**Question:** What should I do before committing to a template and making extensive changes?

**Answer:** You should **preview the template** first to ensure its basic structure and layout align with your specific goals before you begin the customization process.
