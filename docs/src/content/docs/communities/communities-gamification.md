**Communities Gamification**

Enhancing user engagement and fostering active participation, gamification features within communities introduce points, levels, leaderboards, and rewards. These elements motivate members to contribute quality content and interact with others, creating a vibrant and collaborative environment.

## **Gamification Features**

### **Points**

Members earn points for their interactions:

-   **1 point** is awarded when someone likes a post or comment.

### **Levels**

As members accumulate points, they move up levels:

-   Each level is represented by a blue badge with a number on their avatar and profile.
-   Higher levels are achieved by gaining more points.

### **Leaderboards**

Leaderboards display the most active members:

-   **7 Days, 30 Days, and Overall:** View members who have earned the most points within these time frames.

### **Rewards**

Group admins and owners can set offline rewards for members:

-   **Custom Rewards:** Text-based rewards like "Win $500 at level 9" can be added.
-   **Achievement Descriptions:** Specify what members need to do to earn the rewards.

## **How to Use Gamification Features**.

## FAQs




**Question:** How do members earn points in the community?
**Answer:** Members earn points through their interactions within the community. For example, 1 point is awarded whenever someone likes a post or comment made by a member.




**Question:** What are the levels, and how can members advance through them?
**Answer:** Levels are achieved by accumulating points. Each level is represented by a blue badge with a number that appears on the member’s avatar and profile. Members advance to higher levels by gaining more points through active participation and engagement.




**Question:** What is the purpose of the leaderboards, and how are they organized?
**Answer:** Leaderboards display the most active members in the community, encouraging participation and engagement. Members can view leaderboards filtered by 7 days, 30 days, and overall activity, showing who has earned the most points within these time frames.




**Question:** How can group admins set up and manage rewards?
**Answer:** Group admins and owners can add rewards by navigating to the rewards tab in group settings. Here, they can create custom text-based rewards, specify achievements needed to earn each reward and customize level names if desired. These rewards are then visible to community members.




**Question:** Can rewards and levels be customized to fit the community’s theme?
**Answer:** Yes, admins can customize level names to align with the community’s theme, making the gamification experience more personalized and engaging for members. They can also create unique text-based rewards that are relevant and motivating for the community.




**Question:** How do I set up the Level Unlock feature for course access?
**Answer:** To set up the Level Unlock feature, go to your community settings, navigate to the course access section, and define the leaderboard level required for members to unlock a specific course.




**Question:** How can I encourage more active participation in my community using gamification features?
**Answer:** To encourage more active participation, group admins can use rewards and achievements to motivate members. Setting achievable and attractive rewards, regularly updating them, and recognizing top contributors can incentivize members to engage more frequently. Additionally, monitoring the leaderboards helps identify what drives engagement within the community.




**Question:** Can admins track which activities drive the most engagement?
**Answer:** Yes, by monitoring points earned, leaderboard standings, and reward claims, admins can analyze member activity to understand what motivates engagement in the community.




**Question:** Can I set different rewards for different groups within the same community?

**Answer:** Rewards are set at the **Group level**. This means if you have multiple groups in your community, you can customize the names of levels and the rewards specifically for each group's theme and goals.
