## **Exact Strategies to Implement**

### **Organizing Leads with Lists:**

**Strategy:** Always create or select a list before starting a search.  
**Implementation:** Navigate to the lead search menu, and create a new list or choose an existing one based on the search type (People, Local, or Domain).

### **Performing a Local Search:**

**Strategy:** Use business type and location filters for targeted results.  
**Implementation:**

1.  Select the Local search method.
2.  Enter the business type (e.g., "realtor") and location (e.g., "Chicago").
3.  Select from the location list and create a new list (e.g., "Chicago Realtors").
4.  Optionally refine your search using a specific business type (e.g., "real estate agency") for a more focused search.

### **Conducting a People Search:**

**Strategy:** Leverage advanced filters to find specific roles and experience levels.  
**Implementation:**

1.  Choose the People search method.
2.  Enter the role (e.g., "real estate valuer") and location.
3.  Apply advanced filters (e.g., "CEO," "management level," "years of experience").
4.  Click continue to view and manually add results to your selected list.

### **Executing a Domain Search:**

**Strategy:** Focus on leads within specific companies.  
**Implementation:**

1.  Select the Domain search method.
2.  Enter the domain (e.g., "tesla.com").
3.  Create or select a list for the results.
4.  Add the leads to the list.

### **Refining Searches for Better Results:**

**Strategy:** Use specific categories and filters to fine-tune your searches.  
**Implementation:**

-   In a Local search, select relevant categories to get more accurate results.
-   In a People search, combine role-specific filters with company size and revenue to target the right audience.

## FAQs




**Question:** What are the three search types available on this platform?
**Answer:** The platform offers three search types: People Search, Local Search, and Domain Search. Each type is designed to target different lead categories.




**Question:** How can I organize my leads using lists?
**Answer:** Leads are saved into unique lists based on the search type. Always create or select a list before or during your search to ensure your leads are properly organized.




**Question:** How can I expand my results in an Industry Search?
**Answer:** To expand your Industry search results, start at a small geographic area like a suburb, and click "Load more" to see additional results. You can also add nearby locations to your search for broader coverage.




**Question:** What filters should I use to refine a People Search?
**Answer:** For more precise results, apply advanced filters like role, management level, company size, and years of experience. This will help you target leads that match your specific criteria.




**Question:** How do I perform a Domain Search for specific companies?
**Answer:** Use the Domain search method by entering a company’s domain name (e.g., "example.com"). This will allow you to find key contacts within that company and add them to a dedicated list.  




**Question:** Can I use the same list for multiple search types?
**Answer:** No, each search type (People, Local, Domain) requires its **own dedicated list** to keep leads organized correctly.




**Question:** What should I do before starting a search?
**Answer:** Always create or select a list for the results. This ensures leads are organized correctly and prevents mixing results from different search types.




**Question:** How can I narrow down my search results for more precise targeting?

**Answer:** You can refine your search by utilizing specific categories and advanced filters. For example, in a People Search, you can combine role-specific filters with company size and revenue, while in a Local Search, selecting specific business categories helps generate more accurate results.




**Question:** How do I view and save the results found in a People Search?

**Answer:** After entering the role and location and applying your filters, you must click **"Continue"** to view the results. From that screen, you can manually select and add the specific leads you want to your chosen list.




**Question:** If I am looking for businesses in a specific city, like "Chicago Realtors," which search method should I use?
**Answer:** You should use the **Local Search** method. This search type is specifically designed to find businesses by entering an industry or business type (e.g., "realtor") along with a geographic area or location (e.g., "Chicago").
